Murder at the Excelsior Hotel

Premise: You wake up in a blood-spattered hotel room holding a murder weapon. Solve the mystery before the police catch you.
Players: 1–4 | Difficulty: Medium | Length: ~90 min
Primary challenge: Clue gathering + managing Aloof "innocent" enemies via Parley

Key Mechanics

  • Hotel Guests — Aloof enemies that patrol between locations. They add doom when they reach Crime Scenes. Discard them via Parley (intellect or willpower 3 test).
  • Driven to Madness — Treachery that makes a guest hostile (loses Aloof, can't Parley). You must fight or evade.
  • Innocents & the chaos bag — Killing innocent enemies poisons chaos tokens. Avoid killing if possible — Parley or evade instead.
  • Doom pressure — Guests add doom, agenda advances fast. Speed matters.
  • Clues to advance — Acts require clues. Investigation is the primary path forward.

Strategy Tips

  • Parley is king. Discarding guests via Parley avoids doom AND avoids poisoning the chaos bag. Prioritize investigators with intellect or willpower 3+.
  • Fine Clothes (+1 to Parley) is one of the best cards for this scenario if you can take it.
  • Don't kill innocents unless forced. Each kill makes the chaos bag worse for the rest of the game.
  • Move efficiently. The hotel has many locations — wasted movement = wasted time against the doom clock.
  • Evade hostile guests rather than killing them. Driven to Madness discards itself when you evade the attached enemy.
  • Clue burst cards shine. Deduction, "Look what I found!", Seeking Answers — anything that grabs multiple clues per action.
  • Bring at least one fighter. Driven to Madness makes enemies hostile and un-Parley-able. Without combat capability, you'll waste actions evading repeatedly. Zoey, Agnes, or Nathaniel cover this.
⚠️ Common mistakes:
  • Engaging Aloof enemies before Parleying — you don't need to! Parley works at your location without engagement.
  • Killing Hotel Guests to "deal with them" — this poisons your chaos bag permanently.
  • Ignoring guests that patrol toward Crime Scenes — they add doom when they arrive.

Recommended Teams (3 players)

Team 1: Daisy Walker + Agatha Crane + Lucius Galloway — Daisy and Agatha dominate Parley and clues; Agatha seals bad tokens; Lucius evades hostile guests without killing
Team 2: Gloria Goldberg + Rex Murphy + Roland Banks — Gloria prevents Driven to Madness via encounter manipulation; Rex vacuums clues; Roland handles the few enemies that must be fought
Team 3: Harvey Walters + Jacqueline Fine + Jenny Barnes — Harvey fuels card draw; Jacqueline cancels bad tokens from poisoned bag; Jenny's resources and flexibility cover gaps

The Blob That Ate Everything

Premise: A massive alien organism is consuming everything in its path. Stop it before it devours Arkham.
Players: 1–4 (designed for large groups) | Difficulty: Hard | Length: ~120 min
Primary challenge: Sustained high damage output against swarms + a massive boss

Key Mechanics

  • The Blob — Massive HP enemy that grows over time. Requires coordinated team damage.
  • Enemy swarms — Constant stream of smaller enemies that distract from the main threat.
  • Damage to advance — Acts advance by dealing enough total damage, not by gathering clues.
  • Scaling difficulty — More players = more enemies, but also more actions.
  • Limited locations — Tight map means enemies stack up at your location.

Strategy Tips

  • Bring weapons. Lots of weapons. Every investigator should have some combat capability.
  • Action economy matters. Free damage (Counterpunch, Guard Dog, retaliatory effects) is premium.
  • Don't over-invest in investigation. Clue requirements are minimal.
  • Burst damage beats sustained pings. Enemies have moderate-to-high HP.
  • Healing and soak matter. Long scenario drains health/sanity.
  • Coordinate targets. Focus fire on one enemy at a time.
⚠️ Common mistakes:
  • Building a pure clue deck — nothing to contribute in combat.
  • Ignoring small enemies to focus on the Blob — they'll overwhelm you.
  • Not bringing enough ammo/charges — sustained scenarios drain limited-use weapons.

Recommended Teams (3 players)

Team 1: Nathaniel Cho + Daniela Reyes + Agnes Baker — Cho bursts with events, Daniela tanks and retaliates against swarms, Agnes adds free damage pings and spell-based fighting
Team 2: Zoey Samaras + Michael McGlen + Lily Chen — Zoey generates resources from engagement, McGlen delivers burst Firearm damage to boss, Lily's stances handle swarm cleanup
Team 3: Daniela Reyes + Jacqueline Fine + Marion Tavares — Daniela absorbs hits and retaliates, Jacqueline ensures spell attacks land on boss, Marion's event-readying gives extra weapon activations

Film Fatale

Premise: Chaos erupts on three different movie sets — horror, prehistoric, and sci-fi. Quell the madness before it spills into reality.
Players: 1–4 | Difficulty: Medium | Length: ~90 min
Primary challenge: Investigation + managing multiple branching locations with moderate enemies

Key Mechanics

  • Multiple movie sets — Different locations with different themes. You may need to split up.
  • Clue-heavy advancement — Acts require clues to progress.
  • Moderate enemies — Not a combat gauntlet, but enemies need handling.
  • Narrative choices — Decisions affect which path you take.
  • Replayability — Different movie sets as "main feature" change the scenario.

Strategy Tips

  • Prioritize clue efficiency. Multi-clue cards (Deduction, Pilfer, Rite of Seeking) are premium.
  • Bring some combat. One dedicated fighter keeps enemies from stalling your clue-getters.
  • Movement matters. Multiple sets = lots of locations. Free movement saves tempo.
  • Balance your team. Flex investigators (Agnes, Jenny, Winifred) shine here.
  • Don't split up too early. Concentrate efforts, clear one area, then move on.
⚠️ Common mistakes:
  • Over-specializing in combat — you'll fall behind on clues.
  • Spreading across all locations at once — concentrate, then expand.
  • Ignoring enemies entirely — they'll stack up and overwhelm you.

Recommended Teams (3 players)

Team 1: Rex Murphy + Monterey Jack + Roland Banks — Rex and Monterey split clue duties with movement efficiency; Roland handles all enemies and contributes clues on kills
Team 2: Daisy Walker + Lucius Galloway + Zoey Samaras — Daisy dominates clue-heavy locations; Lucius evades and discovers across sets; Zoey protects both with combat
Team 3: Harvey Walters + Jenny Barnes + "Ashcan" Pete — Harvey accelerates everyone's draw; Jenny's resources and flexibility cover gaps; Pete's Duke investigates and fights as needed
