⭐ Survivor: "Ashcan" Pete

The Drifter — Duke does everything

"Ashcan" Pete — The Drifter

🧠 Will 4 📖 Int 2 👊 Combat 2 🦶 Agility 3 ❤️ 6 HP 🧠 5 Sanity

Ability: You begin the game with Duke in play. Fast — Discard a card from your hand: Ready an asset you control. (Limit once per round.)

Elder Sign: +2. Ready Duke.

Deckbuilding: Survivor 0–5, Neutral 0–5, up to 5 other level 0 cards from any class.

Duke (Asset, Ally) — Starts in play. Exhaust: Fight at base combat 4, +1 damage. OR Exhaust: Investigate at base intellect 4, may move to a connecting location first. Duke IS Pete's stats.
Wracked by Nightmares (Treachery) — Revelation: Exhaust all your assets. Assets you control cannot ready. Costs 2 actions to discard. Devastating — shuts down Duke entirely.

Tips for Playing Pete

  • 🎯 Mulligan for: Dark Horse or Peter Sylvestre. Duke is always in play (signature), so Pete's setup priority is his economy engine. Dark Horse turns his "stay poor" strategy into +1 to all stats. Peter Sylvestre soaks horror (Pete's 5 sanity is fragile) + gives passive agility.
  • Duke is your entire character. Pete's printed stats (2/2) are irrelevant. Duke fights at 4 combat +1 damage AND investigates at 4 intellect. Protect Duke at all costs.
  • Duke can move + investigate in one action. Exhaust Duke to move to a connecting location and then investigate there. This is insane action efficiency.
  • Pete's ability readies Duke. Discard a card → ready Duke → use Duke again. Two Duke actions per turn (fight + investigate, or investigate twice).
  • Dark Horse + Fire Axe = stay broke on purpose. At 0 resources: Dark Horse gives +1 to all stats (Duke at 5/5!), Fire Axe gets +2 combat for free. Spend resources down before testing.
  • Scavenging recurs items from discard. Succeed by 2 on an investigate (Duke at 4+ vs shroud 2 = easy) → get back Flashlight, Magnifying Glass, or Fire Axe.
  • Dr. Milan + Duke = economy. Milan gives +1 intellect (Duke at 5) AND 1 resource per successful investigation. But conflicts with Dark Horse (you want 0 resources).
  • Two builds in one deck: Milan build (rich, boost Duke) vs Dark Horse build (broke, all stats boosted). Mulligan decides which game plan you're on.
  • Rabbit's Foot draws on failure. Pete's base stats are terrible — when you test without Duke, you'll fail. Rabbit's Foot turns those failures into card draw.

Mitigating Wracked by Nightmares

Wracked by Nightmares exhausts ALL your assets and prevents them from readying. Duke is dead until you spend 2 actions clearing it.

  • Clear it immediately. 2 actions is brutal but a turn without Duke is worse. Don't delay.
  • Pete's ability doesn't help. It readies an asset, but Wracked prevents readying entirely. You MUST discard it.
  • Thin your deck. The faster you draw through, the sooner you see it and get it over with. Better early (low pressure) than late (critical moment).
  • Lucky! can't save you. It's a Revelation — no test to pass or fail. You just eat it.
  • Plan for the lost turn. If you're in a safe location with no enemies, it's less painful. Position before it hits.

Deck Strategy Notes

  • Mulligan for: Dark Horse or Fire Axe. Duke starts in play — you need the stat boost and backup weapon.
  • Duke fights twice per turn. Exhaust Duke (fight at 4, +1 damage) → discard a card to ready Duke → fight again. 4 damage/turn baseline.
  • Dark Horse + Fire Axe at 0 resources: Duke at combat 5, Fire Axe at combat 4 +1 damage. Three fight actions per turn.
  • Dynamite Blast clears swarms. 3 damage to EACH enemy at a location. Blob spawns clusters — Dynamite wipes them.
  • Sneak Attack + evade combo: Evade an enemy (exhausts it) → Sneak Attack deals 2 damage. No fight test needed.
  • Oops! on failed fights: Miss with Fire Axe? Oops! still deals the damage. Fail-forward combat.
  • Lucky! Lv 2 from discard: Even after you play it, you can play it again from your discard pile. Always available for critical tests.
  • Survival Instinct Lv 2: Evade + disengage ALL enemies + move away. Emergency escape when swarmed.
  • Overpower + Manual Dexterity commit to Duke's fights and draw cards — fueling the discard-to-ready engine.
