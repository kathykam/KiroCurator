🚢 Consternation on the Constellation

Spoiler-free strategy guide — Homebrew by Mythos Busters

Premise: Race cultists to find an artifact aboard a sinking cruise ship, dealing with deep ones and the dangers of the ocean.
Players: 1–4 | Difficulty: Hard | Length: ~2–3 hours
Primary challenge: Multiple simultaneous objectives on a large, sinking map with time pressure

Key Mechanics

  • Opening test — Either defeat an enemy without items OR gather clues + Parley with intellect. Determines your starting position.
  • Sinking ship — Locations exhaust over time (representing flooding). Once exhausted, they're harder or impossible to use. Creates urgency.
  • Custom chaos bag — Starts terrible (lots of negatives, no symbol tokens). Improves as you complete side tasks. Symbol tokens here are GOOD.
  • Multiple objectives — Clues, chaos bag improvement tasks, fighting doom-adding cultists, finding the artifact. Can't do everything.
  • Large map — The ship has many decks/locations. Movement efficiency is critical.
  • Diverging paths — Different climax depending on whether you reach a key goal in time.

Strategy Tips

  • Flexibility wins. You need investigators who can handle multiple test types — not hyper-specialists.
  • Movement is critical. The ship is huge and sinking. Wasted movement = flooded locations = lost opportunities.
  • Improve the chaos bag early. Side tasks that add symbol tokens make every subsequent test easier. Front-load these.
  • Don't ignore cultists. They add doom. Let them accumulate and the agenda advances before you're ready.
  • Split objectives wisely. One investigator on chaos bag tasks, one on clues, one on cultists. Coordinate.
  • The opening test matters. Bring at least one investigator who can pass the Parley (intellect) path reliably.
⚠️ Common mistakes:
  • Focusing only on the artifact and ignoring chaos bag improvement — you'll fail tests all game.
  • Not managing doom from cultists — the agenda sneaks up on you.
  • Backtracking across the ship — plan your route, locations won't wait.

Recommended Teams (3 players)

🃏 Team 1 (ideal): Jenny + Agnes + Roland — Jenny funds everything + Parley, Agnes handles willpower tests + spell combat for cultists, Roland fights + Crowbar clues. All built decks, balanced for multi-objective.
🃏 Team 2 (alternate): Roland + Jenny + Pete — Roland fights cultists + Crowbar clues. Jenny's resources solve anything. Pete flexes with Duke (investigate 4, fight 4) between multiple objectives + Will 4 handles treacheries.
🌟 Team 3 (theoretical best): Jenny + Gloria + Norman — Jenny funds everything. Gloria manipulates the encounter deck to control cultist spawns + doom pressure. Norman (Int 5 + Mystic spell access) handles investigation objectives + flexible combat. Build decks for: Gloria, Norman.

Recommended Teams (2 players)

🃏 Specialist: Roland + Rex — Roland fights cultists + gets free clues on kill, Rex vacuums clues for objectives. Clue velocity handles multiple objectives fast.
🃏 Generalist: Pete + Agnes — Pete flexes between objectives with Duke (fight 4, investigate 4). Agnes (Will 5) handles willpower treacheries + spell combat for cultists.
⚡ Synergy: Jenny + Agnes — Jenny's compounding economy funds Agnes's expensive spells. Agnes's Will 5 covers Jenny's Will 3 weakness on treacheries. Multi-objective scenario rewards Jenny's "money solves any problem" approach.
