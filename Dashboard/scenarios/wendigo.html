🏔️ Against the Wendigo

Spoiler-free strategy guide — Homebrew by Vinn Quest

Premise: Explore the wild Hanninah Valley in Canada's far Northwest, searching for missing students from Dr. Nadelmann's expedition.
Players: 1–4 | Difficulty: Hard | Length: ~3–4 hours
Primary challenge: High-shroud exploration across a vast wilderness with river navigation and a deadly boss

Key Mechanics

  • High shroud locations (3–4) — You need intellect 4+ or alternative clue methods to keep pace.
  • River navigation — Moving along the river costs 2 actions. Movement tech (Pathfinder, Shortcut, free moves) is premium.
  • Vast map with side quests — Guides, RCMP, ancient stones. Lots to explore, can't do it all.
  • Wildlife enemies — Moderate threats that appear throughout. Not a combat gauntlet but you need to handle them.
  • The Wendigo — Appears if you fail at clue gathering. High-threat boss enemy.
  • Long scenario — Economy and sustain matter. Card draw and resource generation keep you going.

Strategy Tips

  • Intellect is king. Shroud 3–4 means investigators with int 4+ dominate. Bring Magnifying Glass, Higher Education, or spell-based investigation.
  • Movement efficiency wins. The map is huge. Free moves (Pathfinder, Shortcut, Monterey Jack's ability) save entire turns.
  • Don't try to do everything. Side quests are tempting but the scenario is long enough already. Prioritize the main objective.
  • Bring sustain. 4 hours means you'll cycle your deck. Card draw and resource generation keep you effective late-game.
  • One fighter is enough. Wildlife is moderate. One investigator with combat 4+ handles it while others investigate.
⚠️ Common mistakes:
  • Bringing low-intellect investigators without alternative clue methods — you'll stall on shroud 4.
  • Wasting actions on river movement without movement tech — 2 actions per river move adds up fast.
  • Splitting up too aggressively on the large map — enemies can pick off isolated investigators.

Recommended Teams (3 players)

🃏 Team 1 (ready to play): Rex + Jenny + Roland — Rex handles shroud 3-4 clues, Jenny's resources sustain the 4-hour game + movement tech, Roland fights wildlife + Crowbar investigates. All built decks.
Team 2: Rex + Monterey + Roland — Speed team. Rex and Monterey devour clues across the huge map with movement efficiency. Roland handles wildlife + bonus clues from kills.
Team 3: Norman + Pete + Jenny — Endurance team. Norman's int 5 handles shroud 4, Pete flexes Duke-clues + combat, Jenny's resources sustain the long haul. No Guardian needed.
