📘 Starter Deck Tips

Tips for playing the 5 investigator starter decks out of the box + standalone upgrade paths (9 XP)

Nathaniel Cho — The Boxer Guardian

⚡ Will 3 📖 Int 2 ⚔️ Com 5 🏃 Agi 2 ❤️ 9 🧠 6
Ability: When you deal damage via an event or fight ability on an event → +1 damage. (Once per phase.)
Elder Sign: +1. If attack succeeds, return an event from discard to hand.
Sig: Randall Cho (ally — heal 3 or tutor a Weapon). Weakness: Tommy Malloy (hunter, damage reduced to 1).

How to Play

  • Fight with events, not just weapons. One-Two Punch, Counterpunch, Monster Slayer — each triggers +1 damage from your ability.
  • Boxing Gloves are your engine. Defeat enemy with fight event → search for next Spirit event. Chain kills together.
  • Mulligan for Randall Cho. He tutors Boxing Gloves or .45 Automatic directly into play.
  • Counterpunch is free damage. Fast — play when an enemy attacks you. No action cost + triggers your +1 damage.
  • Once per phase, not per round. You can get +1 damage in Mythos (Counterpunch on attack) AND Investigation (One-Two Punch on your turn).
  • Tommy Malloy: Don't waste events on him (damage reduced to 1 anyway). Basic fight actions or just evade and ignore.
  • Weakness: low intellect (2). Rely on Evidence! and Scene of the Crime for clues, or let teammates investigate.

Harvey Walters — The Professor Seeker

⚡ Will 4 📖 Int 5 ⚔️ Com 1 🏃 Agi 2 ❤️ 7 🧠 8
Ability: After an investigator at your location draws cards during investigation phase → draw 1 more. (Once per round.)
Elder Sign: +1. Draw 1 card.
Sig: Vault of Knowledge (+2 max hand size, exhaust after investigate to let another investigator draw). Weakness: Thrice-Damned Curiosity (1 damage per 3 cards in hand).

How to Play

  • Your hand IS your power. Harvey wants a massive hand — more cards = more commits, more options. Vault of Knowledge raises your max to 10.
  • Intellect 5 is elite. You investigate at 5 base, making most locations trivial. Stack Magnifying Glass for overkill.
  • Your ability triggers off ANY card draw during investigation phase. Upkeep draw doesn't count, but Perception's "draw on success" does. Coordinate with teammates.
  • Combat 1 is the worst in the game. You CANNOT fight. Bring Disc of Itzamna, "I've got a plan!", or rely entirely on teammates for enemies.
  • Thrice-Damned Curiosity punishes big hands. With 9 cards = 3 damage. Play cards aggressively before it shows up, or keep hand at 5-6 to limit damage to 1-2.
  • Vault of Knowledge helps teammates. After you investigate, exhaust it to let another investigator at your location draw. Great support card.

Winifred Habbamock — The Aviatrix Rogue

⚡ Will 1 📖 Int 3 ⚔️ Com 3 🏃 Agi 5 ❤️ 8 🧠 7
Ability: If 2 different non-weakness cards committed to a test → draw 1 card. (Once per test.)
Elder Sign: +1. After test ends, for every 2 you succeeded by, return a committed card to hand.
Sig: Anything You Can Do, Better (commit-only skill, wild icons). Weakness: Arrogance (must commit, SUBTRACTS icons).

How to Play

  • Commit 2 cards to every test. This triggers your draw ability — you spend 2 cards but draw 1 back. Net cost: 1 card for a massive skill boost.
  • Agility 5 makes you the best evader in the game. Evade everything. Don't fight unless you have to.
  • Over-succeed to get cards back. Elder Sign returns committed cards if you succeed by 2+. With agility 5 + 2 committed cards, you often succeed by 4+ and get both back.
  • Willpower 1 is catastrophic. Mythos treacheries will destroy you. Pack Manual Dexterity and Unexpected Courage to commit to willpower tests, or accept you'll fail them.
  • Arrogance is brutal. You MUST commit it, and it subtracts icons. Time it on a test you're already crushing (agility 5 + other commits) so the subtraction doesn't matter.
  • Anything You Can Do, Better has wild icons and is perfect for triggering your 2-card commit ability alongside another card.
  • Pickpocketing + Lockpicks are key starter deck cards — investigate with agility instead of intellect, and draw cards on over-success.

Jacqueline Fine — The Psychic Mystic

⚡ Will 5 📖 Int 3 ⚔️ Com 2 🏃 Agi 2 ❤️ 6 🧠 9
Ability: When an investigator at your location reveals chaos token(s) → reveal 2 additional. Cancel 2 non-auto-fail tokens OR 1 auto-fail. (Once per round.)
Elder Sign: +1. If cancelled/ignored, draw 1 card.
Sig: Arbiter of Fates (2nd use of ability per round). Weakness: Dark Future (forced token reveal each turn until Elder Sign appears).

How to Play

  • Use your ability on the most important test each round. Don't waste it on easy tests — save it for critical investigate/fight actions or nasty treacheries.
  • Works for teammates at your location. Position yourself next to whoever is taking the hardest test this round.
  • Willpower 5 + spells = fight and investigate. Shrivelling and Rite of Seeking both use willpower. Jacqueline is a flex investigator.
  • Arbiter of Fates doubles your ability. Get it out early — 2 manipulated tests per round is incredibly powerful.
  • Dark Future locks your ability down. While it's out, you're spending your ability trying to clear it (need Elder Sign to appear). Play aggressively to end scenarios before it stalls you.
  • Crystal Pendulum (in starter deck) rewards you for naming the right token type — pairs perfectly with seeing 3 tokens.
  • If auto-fail appears with 2 bad tokens: Cancel the auto-fail but take the combined modifier of the other 2. Sometimes -7 is better than auto-fail (if you're at +8).

Stella Clark — The Letter Carrier Survivor

⚡ Will 3 📖 Int 2 ⚔️ Com 3 🏃 Agi 4 ❤️ 8 🧠 8
Ability: After you fail a skill test → take 1 additional action this round. (Once per round.)
Elder Sign: +1. You may auto-fail instead to heal 1 damage and 1 horror.
Sig: Neither Rain nor Snow ×3 (if test fails, cancel ALL effects of failure). Weakness: Called by the Mists (difficulty 4+ tests trigger bad effects).

How to Play

  • Failing is your superpower. Stella WANTS to fail one test per round — it gives her a 4th action. Plan one "throwaway" test each turn.
  • Neither Rain nor Snow makes failure free. Commit it to a test you expect to fail → cancel all negative effects → still get your bonus action. Pure upside.
  • "Look what I found!" triggers on failure. Fail an investigate test → discover 2 clues anyway. Stella's main clue tool.
  • Take Heart on a failed test = draw 2 + gain 2 resources. Fail intentionally with Take Heart committed for massive value.
  • Don't fail EVERY test. You still need to succeed at things. Fail one easy test for the bonus action, then use that action productively.
  • Called by the Mists punishes difficulty 4+ tests. Avoid high-difficulty tests when it's out, or clear it quickly. Stella's low stats mean most tests are already hard.
  • Survivor cards reward failure: Lucky!, Live and Learn, Oops! — all trigger on or modify failed tests. Lean into the fail-forward archetype.
  • Elder Sign choice: Auto-fail to heal is great when you're wounded and the test doesn't matter. Free healing + bonus action from the failure.

🎯 Standalone Upgrade Paths (9 XP)

Standalone mode gives 9 XP. "→ Lv X" means upgrade in place (pay XP difference). All verified against actual starter decklists. All added cards come from the same starter box — no other sets needed.

Murder at the Excelsior Hotel

Key facts: Hotel Guests are Aloof enemies with Parley (intellect/willpower 3 test to discard them). They add doom if they reach Crime Scene locations. Driven to Madness makes them hostile (lose Aloof, can't Parley anymore). Killing innocents poisons the chaos bag — avoid it if possible. You need clues to advance acts. Prioritize: Parley support, clue efficiency, and reactive combat for when enemies go hostile.
👥 Best 3-player team: Harvey (Parley + clues) + Jacqueline (flex + token protection) + Winifred (agility clues + non-lethal evasion)
🚫 Avoid: Nathaniel — Int 2 / Will 3 means he can't Parley at all, and killing guests poisons the chaos bag.

The Blob That Ate Everything

Key facts: Epic combat gauntlet. Swarms of enemies + the Blob itself with massive HP. Multiple acts require dealing huge amounts of damage to advance. Minimal investigation needed. Sustained damage output over many rounds is critical. Designed for up to 4 players cooperatively.
👥 Best 3-player team: Nathaniel (primary fighter) + Stella (off-fighter + soak) + Winifred (flex damage + clues)
🚫 Avoid: Harvey — Combat 1 means he can't contribute to the main objective of killing the Blob.
