🔰 Guardian Investigators

Combat specialists who protect the team and smite evil

Zoey Samaras — The Chef

⚡ Will 4 📖 Int 2 ⚔️ Combat 4 🏃 Agility 2 ❤️ 9 HP 🧠 6 Sanity
Ability: After you become engaged with an enemy → gain 1 resource.
Elder Sign: +1. If attack succeeds, deal +1 damage.
Deckbuilding: Guardian 0–5, Neutral 0–5, up to 5 other level 0 cards from any class.
Zoey's Cross (Accessory) — After an enemy engages you, exhaust + spend 1 resource: deal 1 damage to that enemy. Essentially free chip damage every time something comes at you.
Smite the Wicked — Spawns a random enemy at the farthest location from you. If it's still in play when the game ends, take 1 mental trauma.

Tips for Playing Zoey

  • Embrace engagement. Unlike most investigators who dread enemies, Zoey profits from them. Let enemies come to you — each one is a free resource.
  • Zoey's Cross + her ability = 2 benefits per engagement. You gain 1 resource AND deal 1 damage (spending that resource). Net cost: zero. Net gain: 1 damage on the enemy before you even act.
  • Weapons are priority #1. With 4 combat, even a Machete makes you terrifying. Get a weapon out in the first 2 turns.
  • Low intellect (2) means don't investigate alone. Use Flashlight, "Look what I found!" (splash), or let your Seeker handle clues. Your job is to kill things and protect the team.
  • Low sanity (6) is your Achilles' heel. Prioritize an Ally with sanity soak (Beat Cop, Guard Dog). Horror-heavy scenarios are dangerous for Zoey.
  • Her 5 off-class slots are flexible. Consider: "Look what I found!" (Survivor, clues), Elusive (Rogue, escape), or Peter Sylvestre (Survivor, sanity soak).

Mitigating Smite the Wicked

The enemy spawns far away, so you need to get there. Plan for it:

  • Include movement cards (Shortcut, Pathfinder) to reach it quickly.
  • If the enemy is weak, it's actually a bonus — free resource from engagement + easy kill.
  • If it's a tough enemy far away, prioritize it before the scenario ends. The mental trauma is permanent.
  • In multiplayer, ask a teammate near that location to engage it so it doesn't wander off.

Deck Strategy Notes

  • Mulligan for: Machete or .45 Automatic. Zoey without a weapon is wasted potential. Prepared for the Worst is your backup tutor if you miss.
  • Machete is your bread and butter. With 4 combat + Machete's +1 (when engaged with only one enemy) = 5 combat and +1 damage. Most enemies die in 1–2 hits.
  • Guard Dog is dual-purpose. It soaks damage AND deals 1 damage back when hit. Combined with Zoey's resource-on-engage, enemies are punished just for showing up.
  • Evidence! gives you free clues. After defeating an enemy, discover 1 clue at that location. This is Zoey's main way to contribute to clue-gathering without intellect.
  • Dodge is your safety net. Cancel an enemy attack entirely — use it when a big enemy would deal lethal damage or horror.
  • Charisma lets you run 2 allies. Beat Cop + Guard Dog together is a powerful combo: +1 combat, damage soak, and retaliatory damage.
  • Brand of Cthugha uses willpower (4) to fight. This is huge for Zoey — it bypasses her combat stat entirely and gives her a spell-based attack option.
  • Bandages + Emergency Aid keep your allies alive longer. Heal damage off Guard Dog or Beat Cop to extend their value.
  • Vicious Blow + Overpower are your commit cards. Vicious Blow adds +1 damage on success; Overpower draws a card. Commit them to guarantee kills.

Upgrade Path (Side Deck)

  • Beat Cop (Lv 2) — Exhaust to deal 1 damage to an enemy at your location. Repeatable ping damage.
  • Brand of Cthugha (Lv 4) — Upgraded spell attack, more charges and damage.
  • Cyclopean Hammer — Massive weapon that can push enemies away. End-game powerhouse.
  • Bruiser — Permanent that gives +1 combat. Makes everything easier.
  • Gang Up — Fight event that gets stronger with allies at your location.
  • On the Hunt — Draw enemies to you (free resources from Zoey's ability!).
  • In the Thick of It — Start with 3 XP but 1 physical trauma. Lets you grab key upgrades immediately.

Nathaniel Cho — The Boxer

⚡ Will 3 📖 Int 2 ⚔️ Combat 5 🏃 Agility 2 ❤️ 9 HP 🧠 6 Sanity
Ability: When you deal damage to an enemy by an event or a fight ability on an event → deal 1 additional damage. (Limit once per phase.)
Elder Sign: +1. If attack succeeds, return an event from your discard pile to your hand.
Deckbuilding: Guardian 0–5, Neutral 0–5 only (no off-class splash).
Randall Cho (Ally) — When he enters play: heal 3 damage OR search your deck/discard for a Weapon asset and play it (paying its cost). A flexible tutor that gets your key weapon out.
Tommy Malloy (Enemy) — Hunter. Prey: Nathaniel only. All damage dealt to Tommy is reduced to 1. He's a punching bag that won't go down easily.

Tips for Playing Nathaniel

  • Events are your bread and butter. Cards like One-Two Punch, Counterpunch, and Monster Slayer all trigger his +1 damage ability. Build your deck around fight events.
  • 5 combat is the highest base in the game. You rarely need boosts to hit — focus on damage output instead.
  • His Elder Sign recycles events. This means your fight events are reusable. Lean into this — every Elder Sign is essentially a free card.
  • Randall Cho is amazing early. Mulligan for him. Playing him turn 1 lets you tutor your best weapon (Boxing Gloves, .45 Automatic) directly into play.
  • Boxing Gloves synergy: After you defeat an enemy with a Fight event, Boxing Gloves let you search for another Spirit event. This creates a chain of fight events.
  • No off-class cards. You're pure Guardian. This means you rely on Guardian's limited clue tools (Evidence!, Scene of the Crime) or your teammates for investigation.
  • Low intellect (2) and willpower (3) make treacheries and investigation tough. Pack Guts and Perseverance for encounter protection.

How Fight Events Work

A fight event is an Event card with a Fight action. Play it from hand (1 action + resource cost), resolve the fight, then it goes to discard — one-shot use.

Example — One-Two Punch:

  1. Spend 1 action, play from hand
  2. Fight at +1 combat → if you succeed, deal 1 damage
  3. Fight again at +2 combat → if you succeed, deal 1 damage
  4. Nathaniel's ability adds +1 damage to the first hit = 3 total damage from 1 action

Key fight events:

  • One-Two Punch — 2 attacks in 1 action
  • Counterpunch — Fast! Play when an enemy attacks you (no action cost)
  • Monster Slayer — +3 combat, +1 damage vs. non-Elite
  • Clean Them Out — Fight + gain resources on success

⚠️ Once per phase: Nathaniel's +1 damage only triggers on the first fight event that deals damage each phase. Two events in the same Investigation phase = only the first gets the bonus.

The cycle: Play event → defeat enemy → Boxing Gloves find next event → Elder Sign returns events from discard. Your hand IS your weapon.

Mitigating Tommy Malloy

Tommy has Hunter and all damage is reduced to 1, so he takes many hits to kill:

  • Check his health on the card — you'll need that many separate attacks (since each hit only does 1).
  • Fight events still trigger Nathaniel's +1 damage, but Tommy reduces the total to 1 anyway. Use basic fight actions to save your events for real threats.
  • He has Hunter, so he'll come to you — don't waste actions moving to him.
  • If you can't kill him quickly, evade him and deal with him later. He's annoying but not lethal.
  • Bonus: engaging Tommy doesn't cost you much since his damage output is low. He's a distraction, not a death sentence.
