🔮 Mystic Investigators

Spellcasters who bend reality using willpower

Agnes Baker — The Waitress

⚡ Will 5 📖 Int 2 ⚔️ Combat 2 🏃 Agility 3 ❤️ 6 HP 🧠 8 Sanity
Ability: After 1 or more horror is placed on Agnes Baker → deal 1 damage to an enemy at your location. (Limit once per phase.)
Elder Sign: +1 for each horror on Agnes Baker.
Deckbuilding: Mystic 0–5, Survivor 0–2, Neutral 0–5.
Heirloom of Hyperborea (Accessory) — After you play a Spell card: draw 1 card. Keeps your hand full as you cast spells, fueling more spells.
Dark Memory (Event) — Place 1 doom on the current agenda (this CAN advance the agenda!). Forced: if in your hand at end of your turn, take 2 horror.

Tips for Playing Agnes

  • Horror is your fuel, not your enemy. Agnes WANTS to take horror — each point deals 1 damage to an enemy. With 8 sanity, you have a deep pool to work with.
  • Forbidden Knowledge is Agnes's best card. Exhaust it to take 1 horror (trigger ability = 1 free damage) and gain 1 resource. It's a damage engine AND economy in one card.
  • Willpower 5 is elite. Shrivelling and Rite of Seeking both use willpower — Agnes fights and investigates at 5 (or higher with Holy Rosary). She's a true flex investigator.
  • Her ability triggers once per PHASE, not per round. That's potentially 4 times per round (Mythos, Investigation, Enemy, Upkeep). If horror lands on you during the Mythos phase AND the Investigation phase, that's 2 free damage.
  • Shrivelling's backlash (take 1 horror on symbol tokens) is actually a BONUS for Agnes. You take horror → deal 1 damage to the enemy you're already attacking. It's extra damage, not a penalty.
  • Holy Rosary is essential. +1 willpower AND 2 sanity soak. It protects you while boosting your spells.
  • Heirloom of Hyperborea keeps your hand full. Play it early. Every Spell you cast draws a card, maintaining your options.
  • Survivor 0–2 splash gives you great options: "Look what I found!" (clues on failed tests), Peter Sylvestre (sanity soak + agility), Lucky! (test insurance).
  • Arcane Initiate finds your spells but adds doom. Play her when the agenda threshold is far away, and discard her (assign damage/horror) before doom matters.

Mitigating Dark Memory

Dark Memory is brutal — 1 doom that can advance the agenda, or 2 horror if you hold it. Either way it hurts.

  • Play it immediately when drawn. 1 doom is bad but manageable. 2 horror every turn is a death spiral. Don't hold it.
  • Time it carefully. If the agenda is about to advance anyway (doom is 1 away from threshold), playing Dark Memory costs you nothing extra — the agenda was advancing regardless.
  • If you draw it with 1 doom left on the agenda: This is the nightmare scenario. You might choose to take the 2 horror instead if the agenda advancing would be catastrophic. Agnes can handle 2 horror better than most (it even triggers her ability!).
  • The 2 horror from holding it DOES trigger Agnes's ability. Silver lining: if there's an enemy at your location, you deal 1 damage while suffering the horror.
  • Thin your deck quickly (Heirloom draws, Preposterous Sketches, card draw) to get Dark Memory out of the way early when doom pressure is low.
