📗 Seeker Investigators

Clue-gathering specialists who unravel mysteries

Rex Murphy — The Reporter

⚡ Will 3 📖 Int 4 ⚔️ Combat 2 🏃 Agility 3 ❤️ 6 HP 🧠 9 Sanity
Ability: After you succeed at a skill test by 2 or more while investigating → discover 1 clue at your location. (Limit once per round — Taboo.)
Elder Sign: +2. You may instead choose to automatically fail to draw 3 cards.
Deckbuilding: Seeker 0–5, Neutral 0–5, up to 5 other level 0 cards from any class. No Fortune cards.
Search for the Truth (Event, cost 1) — Discover 1 clue at your location for each card in your hand. Massive clue burst when your hand is full.
Rex's Curse (Treachery) — After you reveal a chaos token, reveal another. If the second is worse, use it instead. Shuffles back into your deck when you fail a test.

Tips for Playing Rex

  • Succeed by 2 is the magic number. With 4 intellect, you need +2 over shroud. Against shroud 2, you need a total of 4 (already there!). Against shroud 3, you need 5 — one Magnifying Glass does it.
  • Taboo limits his ability to once per round. Make that one investigation count. Use Deduction to grab 3 clues in a single action (1 base + 1 Deduction + 1 Rex ability).
  • Dr. Milan Christopher is your engine. +1 intellect AND 1 resource per successful investigation. He funds everything.
  • Shortcut is incredible on Rex. Free movement means more actions for investigating. Move to a new location, investigate, trigger ability — maximum efficiency.
  • Seeking Answers lets you grab clues from connecting locations without moving there. Pairs beautifully with Rex's ability for remote clue vacuuming.
  • "I've got a plan!" is your emergency combat. With high intellect and resources from Milan, you can one-shot most 3-health enemies. Save it for when your Guardian can't reach you.
  • Scavenging recurs items from discard when you succeed by 2+ (which you do constantly). Get back Flashlights, Magnifying Glasses, or Disc of Itzamna.
  • Elder Sign choice: Usually take the +2. Only auto-fail for 3 cards if you're desperate for cards AND the test doesn't matter (e.g., investigating a location with no clues via Burglary).
  • Rex is a multiplayer star. More players = more clues per location = more value from his ability. In solo, locations often have only 1 clue, making his ability useless.

Mitigating Rex's Curse

The Curse forces a second token draw and takes the worse result. It goes away when you fail a test.

  • Over-invest in skill value. If you're at +4 over the test difficulty, even the second token is unlikely to fail you. Hyperawareness and Inquiring Mind help stack the odds.
  • Don't fear it. The Curse makes you fail slightly more often, but Rex's economy is so strong that one failed test isn't catastrophic.
  • Strategic failure: If you draw it early and want it gone, take a low-stakes test you don't mind failing (investigate a cleared location, or take a basic evade you don't need).
  • It shuffles back in. You'll see it again. Accept it as a tax on Rex's incredible power rather than something to "solve."

Deck Strategy Notes

  • Mulligan for: Dr. Milan Christopher or Magnifying Glass. Getting your intellect boost online early is critical.
  • Working a Hunch is testless clues. No skill test = no Rex's Curse risk, no chaos bag. Just grab a clue. Use it on high-shroud locations or to snag the last clue you need to advance.
  • Elusive is your panic button. Disengage from all enemies and teleport to safety.
  • Perception + Inquiring Mind stack your intellect sky-high for that crucial "succeed by 2" threshold.
  • Preposterous Sketches refills your hand for Search for the Truth burst turns.
  • 2× Emergency Cache fuels Hyperawareness boosts and keeps you playing cards without tempo loss.

📊 Deck Analysis

Scenario target: Murder at the Excelsior Hotel (solo)

✅ Strengths
  • Clue compression: Deduction + Rex ability = 3 clues per action. Seeking Answers adds testless remote clues.
  • Shortcut is perfect for Excelsior's multi-room layout — saves actions moving between hotel rooms.
  • Inquiring Mind is always live in a clue-heavy scenario where locations stay loaded.
  • Scavenging + Flashlight loop gives sustained investigation boosts on high-shroud locations.
  • "I've got a plan!" provides emergency combat using Rex's high intellect.
⚠️ Weaknesses
  • Burglary is counterproductive. Costs an action to play, an action to use, and you don't discover clues — actively fights Rex's ability in a clue-race scenario.
  • Light economy. Only 1× Emergency Cache with Milan at cost 4 and "I've got a plan!" needing resources. If Milan doesn't show early, you stall.
  • Thin card draw. Only Preposterous Sketches (cost 2) and Perception cantrips. Hard to find key cards fast in solo.
  • Enemy management is fragile. Just 2× "I've got a plan!" + 2× Elusive. Multiple enemies in one round can lock you down.
  • Manual Dexterity is filler. Rex at 3 agility + 2 icons = 5 evade, but Elusive already handles escape better. It's just a cantrip.
