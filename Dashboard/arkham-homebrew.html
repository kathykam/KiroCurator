🃏 Arkham Horror LCG – Homebrew Campaigns & Scenarios

Fan-made content to play while Chapter 1 is out of print · Updated May 2026

★ Best Campaigns

Ages Unwound by Olivia Juliet
Time travel narrative — investigators fight a cult trying to unmake time and reality. Tight, singular focus with high narrative agency.
Narrative Time Travel Full Campaign
Why it fits you: Singular narrative focus — every scenario drives the story forward with zero filler. Time-loop structure gives clear goals (Purpose Clarity: high). No grid combat, no app, no per-turn bookkeeping. Drama and agency without systemic chaos. This is the homebrew equivalent of your "Adventurer" pillar.
Cyclopean Foundations by The Beard
Cthulhu-based campaign that feels closest to an official FFG release. Each scenario builds on a focused mechanic that gives it a cohesive theme.
Feels Official Cthulhu 8 Scenarios
Why it fits you: Designed to feel like a real FFG cycle — clean rules, consistent mechanical language, no homebrew jank. Focused mechanic-per-scenario means you always know what you're doing. Narrative escalates from a robbery to a globe-spanning conspiracy. No grid, no app, no fiddly boss AI.
Circus Ex Mortis by The Beard
Shub-Niggurath campaign with circus horror theming. Spooky and tense. Comes with a circus-themed investigator card expansion.
Tense Shub-Niggurath + Player Cards
Why it fits you: Tension from narrative pressure, not token management. Bonus investigator expansion = asymmetric powers (your #1 "like"). The Beard's designs are known for clean rules text. Strong thematic throughline keeps you immersed.
Alice in Wonderland by The Beard
Journey to Wonderland to stop shadows from consuming it. Whimsical writing with clever mechanics. May be on the easier side.
Whimsical Approachable Full Campaign
Why it fits you: Low fiddliness, fast setup. Clever mechanics keep it interesting without overwhelming. Strong narrative voice. May be too easy for a veteran — but "easy + fun" beats "hard + tedious" for your profile.
Jumanji by The Beard
Only 4 scenarios — tight with no filler. Based on the 1995 movie. Board game mechanics cleverly incorporated into the card game.
Short & Tight 4 Scenarios 1995 Film
Why it fits you: 4 scenarios = zero bloat. Every scenario earns its place. Board-game-within-a-card-game concept is mechanically clever without being fiddly. Low commitment — finish in two sessions. Narrative is fun and familiar.
The War of the Worlds by The Beard
Three-scenario campaign based on the H.G. Wells novel and Orson Welles radio drama. Short, solid, no low points.
Weekend Campaign 3 Scenarios Sci-Fi
Why it fits you: Sci-fi setting (your favourite). Three scenarios = zero filler. The Beard's clean design language. No ambition beyond "tell this story well" — tight mechanics, no bloat. Great palate cleanser between bigger campaigns.

◆ Campaigns Worth Considering (with caveats)

Call of the Plaguebearer by Dr. Jack Science
Resident Evil / Reanimator inspired. Repeated use of Arkham locations builds familiarity. Complex but varied challenges.
Veteran Resident Evil 8 Scenarios
Caveat: Strong survival horror narrative hits your Adventurer pillar. But "complex mechanics" is a yellow flag — some scenarios may cross your fiddliness threshold. The RE DNA means resource tension, not spreadsheet management, so likely fine.
The Colour Out of Oz by The Beard
Quest through Oz fighting the corrupting Colour. Multi-goal scenarios with treachery upkeep create shifting board states.
Demanding Multi-Goal Oz Setting
Caveat: Strong narrative and creative setting. But "treachery cards with upkeep" = per-turn maintenance tokens. Brushes against your CPU Rule. Play when you're in the mood to focus.
The Ghosts of Onigawa by Myriad
Set in Japan. Half brilliant down-to-earth horror, half uneven epic scale. Good player card expansion.
Japan Setting Uneven + Player Cards
Caveat: The intimate horror half is exactly your vibe. The "epic scale" half reportedly gets messy (more tokens, more exceptions). Worth it for the good scenarios alone.
Bloodborne: City of the Unseen by aughhh
Like the videogame — challenging, rewarding, lore-rich. Long scenarios with high difficulty.
Hard Bloodborne Long Scenarios
Risk: Long + complex + high difficulty = potential survival grind territory. Repeated death/retry loops may feel like the 7th Continent problem. Only if you loved Bloodborne the videogame and accept the grind.

🎭 Best Standalone Scenarios

The Legend of Sleepy Hollow by The Beard
Take on the Headless Horseman in a haunted hollow. Classic horror short story adapted into a single tight scenario.
Standalone Tight Gothic Horror
Why it fits: Single session, zero bloat. The Beard's clean design. Strong narrative hook (you know the story). No setup overhead — just shuffle and go.
The Fall of the House of Usher by The Beard
Poe's gothic masterpiece as a scenario. The house itself is the antagonist — atmosphere and dread without complex enemy AI.
Standalone Atmospheric Poe
Why it fits: Narrative-driven horror with environmental tension rather than fiddly boss mechanics. No monster AI to run — the house IS the threat. Pure Adventurer pillar.
The Phantom of the Opera by The Beard
Investigate the Paris Opera House. Mystery and drama in a single scenario with a strong narrative arc.
Standalone Mystery Investigation
Why it fits: Investigation-focused (your Detective Stories preference). Single antagonist with clear narrative motivation. No grid, no complex AI. Drama over mechanics.
The Invisible Man by The Beard
H.G. Wells sci-fi horror. Hunt an invisible antagonist through a single tense scenario.
Standalone Sci-Fi Cat & Mouse
Why it fits: Sci-fi setting. Single clear objective. The "invisible" mechanic creates tension through uncertainty, not through token tracking.
The Strange Case of Dr. Jekyll and Mr. Hyde by The Beard
Classic duality horror. Investigate the connection between Jekyll and Hyde in a narrative-driven standalone.
Standalone Gothic Duality
Why it fits: Investigation + narrative. The duality theme creates interesting mechanical choices without complex upkeep. Classic horror literature = strong thematic hook.
The Facts in the Case of M. Valdemar by The Beard
Poe short story adaptation. A man kept alive through mesmerism begins to decay. Intimate horror scenario.
Standalone Poe Body Horror
Why it fits: Intimate, contained horror. Short story = tight scenario with no padding. Atmospheric dread over mechanical complexity.
Delta Green: Convergence by Mr Dodger
Government conspiracy meets Lovecraft. Delta Green RPG scenario adapted for Arkham LCG. Investigation-heavy.
Standalone Conspiracy Investigation
Why it fits: Investigation-focused (Detective Stories preference). Government conspiracy = strong narrative hook. X-Files meets Lovecraft.
The Symphony of Erich Zann by Axolotl
Stop an orchestra from playing otherworldly music. Lovecraft short story with a unique musical theme.
Standalone Lovecraft Unique Theme
Why it fits: Lovecraft source material you love. Unique "stop the music" objective is clear and dramatic. One of the most polished fan standalones — hosted directly on Hall of Arkham.
Arkham Incidents (5-scenario set) by The Beard
Five short standalone scenarios designed to slot between campaign scenarios as side stories. Each is self-contained with unique mechanics.
Standalone Set Modular 5 Scenarios
Why it fits: Designed as palate cleansers — short, tight, no commitment. Play one between campaign scenarios for variety. Each tries something different without overstaying its welcome.
