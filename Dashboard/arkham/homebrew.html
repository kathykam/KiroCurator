🃏 Arkham Horror LCG – Homebrew Campaigns & Scenarios

Fan-made content to play while Chapter 1 is out of print · Updated May 2026

★ Campaigns (Sorted: Mechanics tier → Narrative strength)

#1 · Ages Unwound by Olivia Juliet · Mechanics: 10 · Narrative: 10 · Fiddliness: 1
Time travel narrative — investigators fight a cult trying to unmake time and reality. Tight, singular focus with high narrative agency.
7 Scenarios
Mechanics are weird without being complicated. Zero rules confusion. Narrative is restrained and brilliant: cause-and-effect time loops. Only 7 scenarios — nothing wasted. Zero fiddliness, maximum drama.
#2 · Circus Ex Mortis by The Beard · Mechanics: 9 · Narrative: 9 · Fiddliness: 2
Shub-Niggurath campaign with circus horror theming. Comes with a circus-themed investigator card expansion.
8 Scenarios+ Player Cards
Moon Token mechanic is elegant — one simple rule creating constant tension. Story is frantic and succinct. Bonus investigator expansion = asymmetric powers (your #1 "like").
#3 · Dark Matter by Axolotl · Mechanics: 8 · Narrative: 9 · Fiddliness: 3
Deep space sci-fi horror. Wake from cryo-sleep on an abandoned ship, adventure across the solar system. Spiritual sequel to Path to Carcosa.
8 ScenariosSci-Fi!Owned
SCI-FI SETTING — your #1 favourite. Strong narrative (mystery of your own memories, ancient threat). Choose-your-own-adventure middle section. Comes with a Science Expansion. Fiddliness estimated from community feedback.
#4 · Cyclopean Foundations by The Beard · Mechanics: 9 · Narrative: 7 · Fiddliness: 2
Cthulhu-based campaign that feels closest to an official FFG release. Focused "Non-Euclidean" mechanic ramps elegantly.
8 ScenariosOwned
Mechanically the tightest — zero rules confusion, "smooth" is the defining word. Narrative starts strong but takes a backseat in the second half once the mystery is solved.
#5 · Heart of Darkness by Vinn Quest · Mechanics: 8 · Narrative: 9 · Fiddliness: 3
Travel deep into the Congo aboard the Roi des Belges. Highest-rated campaign on Arkham Central (4.86/5, 29 votes).
Full Campaign
Same creator as Against the Wendigo (community's #1 standalone). Strong exploration narrative. Some translation quirks but nothing per-turn heavy.
#6 · Alice in Wonderland by The Beard · Mechanics: 7 · Narrative: 8 · Fiddliness: 2
Journey to Wonderland to stop shadows from consuming it. Whimsical writing with clever mechanics. Easy side.
8 ScenariosOwned
Narrative is well-written with genuine tonal range. Theme is a personal favourite. Mechanics are clean but simple. Never confusing, never boring, but rarely surprising mechanically. Zero fiddliness.
#7 · The War of the Worlds by The Beard · Mechanics: 7 · Narrative: 7 · Fiddliness: 1
Three-scenario campaign based on H.G. Wells novel and Orson Welles radio drama. Short, solid, no low points.
3 ScenariosSci-Fi
Solid across the board. Sci-fi setting. Zero filler, zero fiddliness. Perfect palate cleanser. Doesn't try anything too fancy.

❌ Campaigns That Didn't Make the Cut

Popular campaigns considered but excluded — too fiddly, mechanics below threshold, or inconsistent quality.

Call of the Plaguebearer by Dr. Jack Science · Mechanics: 7 · Narrative: 8 · Fiddliness: 6
Resident Evil / Reanimator inspired. Repeated use of Arkham locations builds familiarity.
CUT — Fiddliness 6. Strong survival horror narrative but each scenario introduces new mechanics that pile up. Between-scenario overhead adds up across 8 scenarios. Too much bookkeeping.
The Colour Out of Oz by The Beard · Mechanics: 8 · Narrative: 7 · Fiddliness: 7
Quest through Oz fighting the corrupting Colour. Multi-goal scenarios with Decay timers and Pervasive enemies.
CUT — Fiddliness 7 is a dealbreaker. Too much to track every turn: Decay tokens, Pervasive enemies, companions, multi-goal upkeep. "Requires a good, methodical method." Mechanics are good but the overhead kills the fun.
The Ghosts of Onigawa by Myriad · Mechanics: 6 · Narrative: 7 · Fiddliness: 6
Set in Japan. Half brilliant quiet horror, half uneven epic scale. Good player card expansion.
CUT — Mechanics 6 (below threshold). Scenarios 1-3 are excellent but the campaign throws every mechanic at the wall without identity. Second half devolves into slugfest. Ending is busywork.
Darkham Horror by Frostgrave · Mechanics: 7 · Narrative: 6 · Fiddliness: 4
Follows the plot of Darkest Dungeon. Challenge and bleakness of the videogame.
CUT — Back half of campaign flags significantly. Player card expansion is poorly designed. Over-reliance on sticky encounter cards. Narrative below 7 threshold.
The Matter of Britain by Frying Tonight · Mechanics: 7 · Narrative: 7 · Fiddliness: 5
Pulp adventure focused on King Arthur. Extremely ambitious with tons of story paths.
CUT — Some scenarios are brilliant but others falter. Inconsistent quality across the campaign. Ambition exceeds execution in places.
Celtic Rising by Qggone · Mechanics: 6 · Narrative: 5 · Fiddliness: 3
Five scenarios set in Ireland. Introduces a new stat mechanic.
CUT — New stat mechanic falls short. Story text is thin — feels like disconnected side scenarios. Last scenario falls apart. Mechanics 6 below threshold.
The Crown of Egil by analyzechris · Mechanics: 6 · Narrative: 6 · Fiddliness: 3
Four scenarios set in Iceland, centered around a mythological poet/trickster/berserker.
CUT — Interesting mechanical ideas but fails to pay them off at the end. Story is barebones. Feels like it could be tighter. Disappointing conclusion.
Souls of Darkness by JackofHearts · Mechanics: 6 · Narrative: 6 · Fiddliness: 4
Eight scenarios about taking down vampiric blood mages.
CUT — Ambitious and messy. Each scenario tries something new but feels unpolished or too crowded. Lacks consistency.
Bloodborne: City of the Unseen by aughhh · Mechanics: 9 · Narrative: 9 · Fiddliness: 8
Challenging Soulslike campaign. Long scenarios, deep lore, complex mechanics.
CUT — Excellent quality but Fiddliness 8 is extreme. Long scenarios + complex mechanics + high difficulty = exhausting. Not for casual play. Mechanics are great if you can handle the overhead.

🎭 Standalones (Sorted: Mechanics tier → Narrative strength)

#1 · The Fall of the House of Usher by The Beard · Mechanics: 9 · Narrative: 9 · Fiddliness: 2
Poe's gothic masterpiece. The house crumbles around you as doom ticks — locations flip to dangerous alternate sides.
Tightly paced. The house IS the threat, not fiddly monster AI. Novel boss mechanic with multiple paths to participation.
#2 · The Invisible Man by The Beard · Mechanics: 9 · Narrative: 7 · Fiddliness: 1
H.G. Wells sci-fi horror. Hunt an invisible antagonist — flip combat/evasion decks, stack them in your favour.
Sci-Fi
"Simple enough idea that ends up being a ton of fun." Perfect difficulty. Zero fiddliness. Sci-fi setting.
#3 · Consternation on the Constellation by Mythos Busters · Mechanics: 8 · Narrative: 8 · Fiddliness: 3
Race cultists aboard a sinking cruise ship. Chaos bag manipulation, multiple objectives, diverging climax.
Owned
Chaos bag manipulation is clever. Multiple objectives give agency. Ship flooding = escalating tension. Large map can feel sprawling.
#4 · Against the Wendigo by Vinn Quest · Mechanics: 7 · Narrative: 9 · Fiddliness: 3
Investigate a wendigo in the Canadian Rockies. Massive open-world scenario. Community's most-loved fan standalone (4.67/5, 124 votes).
Owned
Universally praised atmosphere. Open exploration with multiple endings. Long (~4 hours). Shares DNA with T.I.M.E Stories and Sleeping Gods — narrative exploration with real consequences. Minor translation artifacts.
#5 · The Legend of Sleepy Hollow by The Beard · Mechanics: 8 · Narrative: 8 · Fiddliness: 1
Take on the Headless Horseman. Multiple spread-out objectives where you choose which to tackle.
Owned
"Straightforward and to the point." Choose-your-own-objectives puzzle. Zero fiddliness. On the easy side.
#6 · The Phantom of the Opera by The Beard · Mechanics: 8 · Narrative: 8 · Fiddliness: 2
Investigate the Paris Opera House using the Concealed mechanic in new ways.
Fresh twist on Concealed mechanic. Investigation-focused. Requires Scarlet Keys familiarity.
#7 · Delta Green: Convergence by Mr Dodger · Mechanics: 7 · Narrative: 8 · Fiddliness: 2
Government conspiracy meets Lovecraft. Investigation-heavy.
Investigation-focused. X-Files meets Lovecraft. Solid but less mechanically inventive.
#8 · The Facts in the Case of M. Valdemar by The Beard · Mechanics: 7 · Narrative: 7 · Fiddliness: 1
Poe short story. A man kept alive through mesmerism begins to decay.
Intimate, contained horror. Zero fiddliness. Atmospheric dread over mechanical complexity.
#9 · The Symphony of Erich Zann by Axolotl · Mechanics: 7 · Narrative: 7 · Fiddliness: 2
Stop an orchestra from playing otherworldly music. Lovecraft short story.
Lovecraft source material. Unique objective. Polished. Solid all around.
#10 · Arkham Incidents (5-scenario set) by The Beard · Mechanics: 6 · Narrative: 6 · Fiddliness: 2
Five short standalones, each involving an iconic ally. Varied mechanics.
Fun palate cleansers but deck-dependent. Best two: "Too Noble" and "Mysterious Benefits."

❌ Didn't Make the Cut

Popular standalones that were considered but excluded (Mechanics below 7, or fundamental design issues).

The Grand Oak Hotel by ArgusTheCat · Mechanics: 6 · Narrative: 7
Hotel mystery with memory-loss "Nobody" mechanic. Cool hook but arrives too late — half the encounter deck is blank cards until midway. Massive difficulty spikes (nothing or instant death). "Extremely uneven."
Dr. Jekyll and Mr. Hyde by The Beard · Mechanics: 6 · Narrative: 6
Variable doom clock with escalating tests. Mechanically straightforward but "doesn't particularly shine." Hyde feels like an annoyance rather than a threat. Lacks a memorable hook.
Blood Spilled in Salem by Colin Towle · Mechanics: 5 · Narrative: 5
Witch ritual in Salem. Arbitrary hand-slot requirement can stall builds. Most locations are filler. Encounter cards extremely uneven. Anti-climactic ending. Wastes its theme.
The Undying by Weyird · Mechanics: 5 · Narrative: 6
Zombie survival with doom/civilian mechanics. Fundamental design flaw: you can (and should) just rush past everything for a quick win. Choosing to engage with the mechanics is suboptimal.
The Colour Out of Space by Joe Ehret · Mechanics: 6 · Narrative: 6
Lovecraft adaptation. Reviewed but didn't stand out mechanically or narratively enough to earn a top-10 spot.
The Nameless City by bau · Mechanics: 6 · Narrative: 6
Exploration scenario. Reviewed but didn't distinguish itself from stronger entries in the same space.
'Tis the Season by Frying Tonight · Mechanics: 6 · Narrative: 6
Holiday-themed scenario. Fun novelty but not mechanically strong enough for the ranked list.
Code Red at Bleeding Heart by Myriad · Mechanics: 5 · Narrative: 6
Haunted hospital with location-damage mechanic. Cool ideas but "don't quite play out as well in practice." Main mechanic falls flat, second half is random chance, hidden enemy has no counterplay for some builds.
Defense of the Arkham by Rei · Mechanics: 5 · Narrative: 5
Tower-defense style scenario. Niche concept that doesn't translate well to the Arkham card game format.
Stranger Things by Mythos Busters · Mechanics: 5 · Narrative: 6
2017 fan scenario based on the TV show. Historically significant (one of the first popular fan scenarios) but mechanically outdated by modern standards.
