📋 Arkham Horror LCG – Official Rankings

FFG campaigns & standalones ranked by profile fit · Updated May 2026
Ownership: Owned Wishlist Not Owned

★ Tier A — Essential

#1 · The Path to Carcosa 2017 · Mechanics: 9 · Narrative: 10 · Fiddliness: 2
Theatrical horror masterpiece. Doubt/conviction mechanic, unreliable narrator, multiple endings. Community consensus #1 campaign.
Owned8 Scenarios
Your Adventurer pillar in its purest form — narrative agency, dramatic stakes, zero fiddliness. The doubt/conviction mechanic IS player choice mattering. Every scenario has a distinct identity.
#2 · The Dunwich Legacy 2017 · Mechanics: 9 · Narrative: 9 · Fiddliness: 2
Classic Lovecraft. Miskatonic University, the Necronomicon, extradimensional horrors. Your first full campaign — rated 10.
Owned8 Scenarios
The benchmark. Every campaign is measured against this. Essex County Express is a top-5 scenario in the entire game. Clean escalation, zero bloat.
#3 · Edge of the Earth 2021 · Mechanics: 8 · Narrative: 9 · Fiddliness: 3
Antarctic expedition horror. Strong partner mechanic, branching narrative, escalating dread.
Owned8 Scenarios
Partners give narrative weight to NPCs. Expedition theme = exploration drama. Slightly more bookkeeping than Dunwich but worth it. Strong investigator pool.

◆ Tier B — Strong

#4 · The Innsmouth Conspiracy 2021 · Mechanics: 8 · Narrative: 9 · Fiddliness: 3
Coastal horror with flooding mechanic and keys. Strong community ranking, tight narrative arc.
Not Owned8 Scenarios
Flooding locations = environmental tension without monster AI. Keys mechanic adds strategic layer. Strong investigators (Amanda, Trish are S-tier). Buy candidate.
#5 · The Circle Undone 2019 · Mechanics: 8 · Narrative: 8 · Fiddliness: 3
Haunted house, witch covens, Masonic conspiracies. Prologue mechanic and tarot cards.
Wishlist8 Scenarios
On your wishlist. Prologue = narrative stakes before the campaign even starts. Tarot adds opportunity luck. Diana Stanley is S-tier.
#6 · The Drowned City 2025 · Mechanics: 7 · Narrative: 8 · Fiddliness: 3
Newest campaign. Underwater Arkham, gang war opening, Cthulhu endgame.
Owned8 Scenarios
Strong second half. Opening scenario is incongruous (gang war with no bearing on narrative) but it finds its footing. Lucius Galloway is a standout investigator.
#7 · The Feast of Hemlock Vale 2024 · Mechanics: 8 · Narrative: 8 · Fiddliness: 4
Folk horror in rural New England. Cottagecore meets cosmic dread. Strong new investigators.
Wishlist8 Scenarios
Kate Winthrop is S-tier. Folk horror is a fresh tone. Slightly more fiddly than ideal but the atmosphere compensates.

○ Tier C — Solid with Caveats

#8 · The Forgotten Age 2018 · Mechanics: 7 · Narrative: 8 · Fiddliness: 5
Jungle expedition. Explore mechanic, supply system, punishing difficulty. Polarizing.
Owned8 Scenarios⚠️ Fiddly
Explore mechanic can feel like systemic chaos — cards invalidating plans. Supply tracking is maintenance. Strong narrative but the mechanics fight your agency.
#9 · The Dream-Eaters 2019 · Mechanics: 7 · Narrative: 7 · Fiddliness: 4
Dual campaign structure — waking world and Dreamlands. Innovative but divisive split.
Not Owned8 Scenarios (split)
Split structure means half the deck-building payoff. Dreamlands tone is lighter. Interesting but fragmented. Tony Morgan and Mandy Thompson are excellent investigators.
#10 · The Scarlet Keys 2022 · Mechanics: 7 · Narrative: 7 · Fiddliness: 5
Open-world globetrotting. Noir detective vibe. Strong first playthrough, weaker on replay.
Not Owned~20 Scenarios⚠️ Fiddly
RNG-based main mechanism gets old. Lots of reading stretches table time. Great blind playthrough, diminishing returns. Strong investigator pool though.
#11 · Night of the Zealot 2016 · Mechanics: 6 · Narrative: 6 · Fiddliness: 1
Core Set tutorial campaign. Three scenarios introducing the game's systems.
Owned3 Scenarios
It's the tutorial. Does its job. The Midnight Masks is genuinely great; the other two are functional. Zero fiddliness saves it from Tier D.

★ Tier A — Essential

#1 · Murder at the Excelsior Hotel 2019 · Mechanics: 10 · Narrative: 10 · Fiddliness: 1
Whodunit in a hotel. Multiple suspects, multiple solutions, massive replayability. You rated it 10.
Owned
Your only 10-rated standalone. Detective narrative with real clues. Zero fiddliness. Replayable. The gold standard for what a standalone can be.

◆ Tier B — Strong

#2 · Carnevale of Horrors 2016 · Mechanics: 8 · Narrative: 8 · Fiddliness: 2
Venetian carnival with a circular location layout. Unique movement mechanic, strong theme.
Wishlist
Circular map is mechanically inventive. Carnival atmosphere is dripping with theme. Unique among all standalones for spatial puzzle.
#3 · Film Fatale 2025 · Mechanics: 7 · Narrative: 8 · Fiddliness: 3
Noir mystery on a film set. Newest official standalone.
Owned
Investigation-focused noir. Solid narrative with good atmosphere. Not as replayable as Excelsior but a strong one-shot.
#4 · The Midwinter Gala 2024 · Mechanics: 7 · Narrative: 7 · Fiddliness: 2
Social deduction at a holiday party. Unique tone for Arkham.
Wishlist
Lighter tone, social mechanics. Interesting experiment that works. Low fiddliness.

○ Tier C — Solid with Caveats

#5 · The Blob That Ate Everything 2019 · Mechanics: 7 · Narrative: 6 · Fiddliness: 3
Epic-scale cooperative battle. Designed for large groups. Campy B-movie tone.
Wishlist
More combat spectacle than narrative. Fun as a group event but not your Adventurer sweet spot. Best with 3-4 players.
#6 · Fortune and Folly 2022 · Mechanics: 6 · Narrative: 6 · Fiddliness: 3
Casino heist scenario. Gambling mechanics and resource management.
Not Owned
Gambling mechanics are opportunity luck but the scenario is mechanically thin. Fun theme, average execution.
#7 · War of the Outer Gods 2021 · Mechanics: 6 · Narrative: 5 · Fiddliness: 4
Three elder god factions battle while investigators try to survive. Chaotic multiplayer.
Not Owned
Faction mechanics add bookkeeping. More spectacle than agency. Can feel like watching the game play itself.
#8 · Machinations Through Time 2021 · Mechanics: 6 · Narrative: 6 · Fiddliness: 5
Time-travel epic designed for multiple groups playing simultaneously. GenCon event scenario.
Not Owned⚠️ Fiddly
Designed for convention play with multiple tables. Loses most of its appeal as a home game. Complex setup for limited payoff solo.
#9 · Labyrinths of Lunacy 2018 · Mechanics: 6 · Narrative: 5 · Fiddliness: 5
Escape room scenario designed for three groups of investigators. Puzzle-heavy.
Not Owned⚠️ Fiddly
Another convention scenario. Requires specific group setup. Puzzle mechanics are interesting but the logistics are a barrier. Single-group mode exists but feels incomplete.
