Enemies & Combat

How do Nathaniel Cho's fight events work?

A fight event is an Event card with a Fight action on it. You play it from hand (1 action), resolve the fight, then it goes to your discard pile — one-shot use.

Example — One-Two Punch:

  1. Spend 1 action, play from hand (pay cost)
  2. Fight at +1 combat. If you succeed → 1 damage
  3. Fight again at +2 combat. If you succeed → 1 damage
  4. Nathaniel's ability adds +1 damage to the first hit that deals damage

Result: 1 action, 2 attacks, up to 3 damage.

⚠️ Key rule: Nathaniel's +1 damage triggers once per phase, not once per event. Two fight events in the same Investigation phase = only the first gets the bonus.

Boxing Gloves search for the next Spirit event after you defeat an enemy with a fight event, creating a chain. His Elder Sign returns events from discard to hand.

Do I need to Engage an Aloof enemy before using Parley on it?

No. You can Parley directly with an Aloof enemy without engaging it first.

  • Aloof means the enemy doesn't automatically engage you. You must Engage it only if you want to attack (Fight) it.
  • Parley only requires the enemy to be at your location — it does not require engagement.
  • This saves you 1 action compared to Engage → Parley.

Relevant in: Murder at the Excelsior Hotel (suspects are Aloof with Parley abilities).

Source: Rules Reference — "Aloof" keyword: "An investigator must use the engage action to engage an aloof enemy in order to attack it."

Does Driven to Madness on a Hotel Guest cause it to immediately engage an investigator?

Yes. Driven to Madness causes the attached enemy to lose Aloof. Once Aloof is removed, normal engagement rules apply — a ready, unengaged enemy at an investigator's location immediately engages them.

Additionally, Driven to Madness says "Investigators cannot parley with attached enemy," so the Hotel Guest's Parley action is completely blocked.

Your options:

  • Fight — the guest is now 2 fight / 2 health / 3 evade.
  • Evade — triggers the Forced effect: "After attached enemy is evaded: Discard Driven to Madness." This restores the guest to normal (Aloof, Parley-able).

⚠️ Best play: Evade to discard Driven to Madness, then Parley the guest on a later turn.

How does Jacqueline Fine's ability work?

When any investigator at her location would reveal chaos token(s), she can activate her ability (once per round) to reveal 2 additional tokens (3 total). Then choose:

  • Cancel 2 non-auto-fail tokens — keep the best one, OR
  • Cancel 1 auto-fail token — but you're stuck with the other 2 tokens combined as your modifier.

Key rules:

  • Works for any investigator at her location, not just herself.
  • Must decide to use it BEFORE tokens are revealed — no peeking.
  • Cancelled tokens are treated as never revealed (no "on reveal" triggers).
  • Her Elder Sign (+1): if cancelled/ignored, draw 1 card.

⚠️ Watch out: If auto-fail appears with two high negatives (e.g., auto-fail/-4/-3), cancelling the auto-fail means taking -7. Sometimes that's worse than just failing.

Arbiter of Fates (signature asset) grants a second use per round.

Dark Future (weakness) locks down her ability while in play — goes away when Elder Sign appears during its forced end-of-turn reveal.

Campaign & Deckbuilding

Do Permanent cards count toward your deck size?

No. Permanent cards do not count toward your 30-card deck size. They are "extra" — outside the deck entirely.

  • Start in play at the beginning of every game (no need to draw them).
  • Cannot be discarded, returned to hand, or removed from play.
  • Are never shuffled into your deck.

⚠️ Common mistake: Players add a Permanent and remove another card to stay at 30. Don't — your deck stays at 30 cards and the Permanent sits in play for free on top of that.
