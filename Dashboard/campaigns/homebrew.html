📖 Homebrew Campaign Guides

Strategy tips for owned fan-made campaigns

🚀 Dark Matter

by Axolotl · 8 scenarios · Mechanics: 8 · Narrative: 9 · Fiddliness: 3

Premise: Deep space sci-fi horror. You wake from cryo-sleep on an abandoned ship and adventure across the solar system. A spiritual sequel to Path to Carcosa in tone.
Players: 1–4 | Difficulty: Medium–Hard | Length: ~14–18 hours (8 scenarios)
Key mechanics: Choose-your-own-adventure middle section (pick which scenarios to play), memory/identity mystery, Science Expansion player cards, space-themed locations
XP available: ~35–45 total

Recommended Teams (3 players)

🃏 Team 1 (prebuilt — best): Zoey + Rex + Agnes — Balanced team for a balanced campaign. Investigation-heavy early (mystery solving), combat-heavy late (threats escalate). Willpower matters for the horror/identity themes — Zoey's Will 4 handles this better than Roland's Will 3 across 8 scenarios. Agnes handles willpower + damage, Rex handles clues, Zoey handles combat + resources.
Team 2: Zoey + Harvey + Agnes — Harvey's card draw keeps the team fueled across the branching middle section. His Int 5 + Will 4 handles high-shroud space locations and willpower tests. More consistent than Rex but less explosive.
Team 3: Zoey + Rex + Jacqueline — Jacqueline for willpower/token control, Rex for speed. Strong defensive team for the horror-heavy back half.

General Strategy

  • The middle section lets you choose scenarios. You pick which locations to visit. All paths are viable — choose based on your team's strengths and what information you want.
  • Investigation dominates the first half. You're solving a mystery. Clue-heavy investigators (Rex, Harvey) are essential early.
  • Combat escalates in the back half. Threats become more dangerous as the mystery unravels. Have upgraded weapons ready by scenario 5-6.
  • Willpower tests are frequent. The identity/horror themes test willpower throughout. Similar to Carcosa — Agnes and Jacqueline excel.
  • The Science Expansion adds player cards. If you use them, they're thematic and balanced. Not required but fun.

🏛️ Cyclopean Foundations

by The Beard · 8 scenarios · Mechanics: 9 · Narrative: 7 · Fiddliness: 2

Premise: Cthulhu-based campaign. Non-Euclidean geometry warps reality around you. Feels closest to an official FFG release in quality and design.
Players: 1–4 | Difficulty: Medium | Length: ~14–18 hours (8 scenarios)
Key mechanics: Non-Euclidean locations (connections change, distances warp), escalating geometry mechanic that ramps elegantly, clean scenario design with zero rules confusion
XP available: ~35–45 total

Recommended Teams (3 players)

🃏 Team 1 (prebuilt — best): Zoey + Rex + Agnes — Balanced campaign needs balanced team. The Non-Euclidean mechanic makes movement unpredictable — you need investigators who can handle whatever they find when connections shift. Zoey's Will 4 + Combat 4 handles surprises. Rex clues, Agnes flexes. Movement cards are extra valuable here.
Team 2: Zoey + Rex + Jenny — Jenny's balanced stats handle the unpredictability of Non-Euclidean connections. Her resources fund movement cards and flexible solutions. Good when you don't know what's coming.
Team 3: Zoey + Harvey + Agnes — Harvey for consistent clues + draw, Agnes for willpower + flex damage. Reliable across all 8 scenarios.

General Strategy

  • Non-Euclidean geometry changes location connections. Locations that were adjacent may no longer be. Plan movement carefully and bring cards that bypass normal movement rules.
  • The mechanic ramps elegantly. Early scenarios introduce it gently. By the back half, the geometry is fully warped. Adapt gradually.
  • Movement cards are premium. Shortcut, Pathfinder, Elusive — anything that ignores normal connections is extremely valuable when geometry shifts.
  • Combat is steady throughout. Not as spikey as Carcosa or Forgotten Age. Consistent combat presence from start to finish.
  • Investigation is straightforward. Clue requirements are fair. Rex handles them easily. No gimmick investigation mechanics.
  • Zero fiddliness. Rules are clean. No per-turn bookkeeping. The mechanic is elegant — one rule creating constant tension.

🐇 Alice in Wonderland

by The Beard · 8 scenarios · Mechanics: 7 · Narrative: 8 · Fiddliness: 2

Premise: Journey to Wonderland to stop shadows from consuming it. Whimsical writing with clever mechanics. On the easier side of campaigns.
Players: 1–4 | Difficulty: Easy–Medium | Length: ~12–16 hours (8 scenarios)
Key mechanics: Whimsical encounters (not all threats are hostile), story choices with tonal range, clean simple mechanics, companion NPCs
XP available: ~35–40 total

Recommended Teams (3 players)

🃏 Team 1 (prebuilt — best): Rex + Jenny + Agnes — Easy campaign with social/Parley focus. No need for a dedicated fighter — Jenny's balanced stats (3/3/3/3) handle social encounters and light combat with Rogue weapons. Agnes (Will 5) dominates Parley and willpower tests. Rex vacuums clues. Relaxed composition for a relaxed campaign.
Team 2: Rex + Jenny + Pete — Pete's Duke (4/4 investigate/fight) + Will 4 handles everything in an easy campaign. Jenny for resources and flexibility. Rex for clue speed. Fun, thematic team.
Team 3: Roland + Harvey + Stella — Stella's Survivor recursion and fail-forward fit the whimsical tone. Harvey for consistent clues. Roland for when things get serious. A fun, thematic team.

General Strategy

  • Not everything is hostile. Wonderland creatures may be interacted with via Parley or other non-combat means. Don't default to fighting.
  • The campaign is on the easier side. Good for a relaxed playthrough or introducing new players. Don't over-optimize — enjoy the story.
  • Story choices matter. The narrative has genuine tonal range. Your decisions affect which Wonderland characters help or hinder you.
  • Willpower and intellect are the primary stats. Social interactions and investigation dominate. Combat is present but not the focus.
  • Companion NPCs travel with you. Similar to Edge of the Earth's partners but simpler. They provide bonuses — keep them alive.
  • Zero fiddliness. Mechanics are clean and simple. Never confusing, never boring. Perfect palate cleanser after Forgotten Age or Drowned City.
