📖 The Forgotten Age

Campaign guide — spoiler-free strategy tips

Premise: An expedition into the ruins of a forgotten civilization. Ancient secrets, deadly traps, and something that should have stayed buried.
Players: 1–4 | Difficulty: Hard (hardest official campaign) | Length: ~16–20 hours (8 scenarios)
Key mechanics: Explore (reveal locations from a deck), Supply Points (choose gear before scenarios), Vengeance (killing certain enemies makes the campaign harder), trauma-heavy
XP available: ~30–40 total (lower than other campaigns due to Vengeance reducing rewards)
⚠️ Campaign Warning: This is the hardest official campaign. It punishes mistakes permanently (trauma carries forward, Vengeance makes the chaos bag worse). Expect investigator deaths. Build resilient decks with healing and horror soak. Don't play this first — play Zealot or Dunwich first.

Recommended Teams (3 players)

🃏 Team 1 (prebuilt — best): Rex + Jenny + Stella — This campaign punishes killing (Vengeance). You want to EVADE, not fight. Jenny (Agi 3 + Streetwise boosts + resources) and Stella (Agi 4 + fail-forward) handle traps brilliantly. Rex explores fast. Stella's Survivor recursion survives the punishing trauma. Jenny with Rogue weapons handles unavoidable combat in the back half. No dedicated Guardian because fighting is actively punished — evade Vengeance enemies instead.
Team 2: Roland + Rex + Jenny — If you want a dedicated fighter for the brutal back half (Scenarios 5-7). Roland's clue-on-kill helps when combat is unavoidable. Jenny handles traps and resources. Trade-off: Roland's Agi 2 fails traps constantly.
Team 3: Rex + Winifred + Stella — Maximum agility. Winifred's Agi 5 trivializes every trap. Her over-succeed draw keeps her hand full. Stella's Agi 4 + fail-forward. Rex for clues. Extremely risky (Winifred's Will 1) but traps never touch this team.

Supply Points — What to Buy

Before certain scenarios, you spend Supply Points on gear. These are campaign resources — choose wisely.

Always buy: Provisions (heal damage/horror between scenarios), Rope/Chalk (bypass specific obstacles)
Situational: Map (helps with Explore), Torches (shroud reduction), Medicine (heal trauma)
Skip: Blanket (marginal benefit), Binoculars (too narrow)

Scenario 1: The Untamed Wilds

Setting: Dense jungle. Your expedition begins.
Primary challenge: Explore mechanic introduction + Vengeance enemies
Doom pressure: Moderate
  • Explore is the new action. Instead of fixed locations, you draw from a location deck. Some draws are dead ends (treacheries). This costs actions — budget for failed explores.
  • Vengeance enemies: DO NOT KILL unless forced. Enemies with Vengeance add to a campaign counter when killed. This counter makes future scenarios harder. Evade them instead.
  • Agility matters immediately. Treacheries test agility for traps. Jenny (3), Winifred (5), and Stella (4) handle these. Roland (2) and Rex (3) struggle.
  • This scenario sets the tone. It's harder than Zealot's opening. Don't be discouraged — the campaign is designed to be punishing from the start.

Scenario 2: The Doom of Eztli

Setting: Ancient temple ruins. Traps everywhere.
Primary challenge: Navigate traps + escape before the temple collapses
Doom pressure: High — timed escape
  • This is a timed escape. You must get in, get what you need, and get out before the agenda advances. Don't linger.
  • Traps test agility and intellect. Failing traps deals damage/horror. Bring agility commits or trap-bypassing cards.
  • Explore continues. Dead-end explores waste precious time. Cards that let you look at the top of the explore deck are valuable.
  • Resign is always an option. If things go badly, resign early. Taking trauma is better than being defeated (which gives MORE trauma).

Scenario 3: Threads of Fate

Setting: Back in Arkham. Multiple objectives compete for your time.
Primary challenge: Multi-objective race — you can't do everything
Doom pressure: High — doom clock is shared across all objectives
  • Multiple storylines run simultaneously. You must choose which objectives to pursue. You cannot complete all of them in one play.
  • Split up. With 3 players, send each investigator to a different objective. Reconvene only if someone needs help.
  • Prioritize based on your campaign state. Some objectives are more valuable depending on earlier choices. Read the act cards carefully.
  • This is the most "open" scenario in the campaign. Enjoy the freedom — later scenarios are more linear and punishing.

Scenario 4: The Boundary Beyond

Setting: Reality shifts. Past and present overlap.
Primary challenge: Locations transform — investigate before they change
Doom pressure: High
  • Locations flip between two states. You need clues from specific sides. Timing your investigation around the flips is key.
  • Vengeance enemies return. Continue evading them. The Vengeance counter is cumulative — every kill makes the rest of the campaign harder.
  • Willpower tests appear more here. The encounter deck shifts toward willpower. Agnes would be useful if she were on your team.
  • Explore is still present. Budget actions for failed explores. Movement efficiency matters.

Scenario 5: Heart of the Elders

Setting: Deep jungle / underground. A two-part scenario.
Primary challenge: Exploration + combat gauntlet
Doom pressure: Moderate per part
  • This is effectively two scenarios. Part 1 is exploration-focused, Part 2 is combat-focused. Your team needs to handle both.
  • Supplies matter here. Rope and Torches have specific uses. If you bought them earlier, they pay off now.
  • Combat ramps significantly. Enemies are tougher than anything you've faced so far. Roland needs upgraded weapons by now.
  • Explore dead ends are extra punishing. The doom clock is tight. Every wasted explore action hurts.

Scenario 6: The City of Archives

Setting: Somewhere very strange. Your abilities are... different.
Primary challenge: Unique puzzle — your normal stats may not apply
Doom pressure: Moderate
  • This scenario changes the rules. Your investigators may not function as expected. Adapt to the new conditions rather than fighting them.
  • Intellect is king here. Investigation and intellect-based tests dominate. Rex and Harvey excel; combat-focused investigators struggle.
  • Don't panic about the setup. The scenario looks weird but it's solvable. Read everything carefully and adapt.
  • This is a breather from the brutality. Enjoy the puzzle — the next two scenarios are the hardest in the campaign.

Scenario 7: The Depths of Yoth

Setting: Deep underground. Descending into darkness.
Primary challenge: Survival descent — each level is harder than the last
Doom pressure: Extreme — Vengeance counter directly affects difficulty here
  • Your Vengeance counter determines how hard this is. Every Vengeance enemy you killed earlier makes this scenario more punishing. This is the payoff for evading instead of killing.
  • Each level is harder. You descend through increasingly dangerous areas. Save your best cards and resources for the deeper levels.
  • Combat is unavoidable. Unlike earlier scenarios, you must fight here. Upgraded weapons are essential.
  • Healing and soak are critical. You'll take damage and horror. Investigators without soak may be defeated.
  • This is widely considered the hardest scenario in the game. Losses are expected. Don't restart — push through.

Scenario 8: Shattered Aeons

Setting: Beyond time. The finale.
Primary challenge: Navigate a fractured reality + make final choices
Doom pressure: High
  • Multiple paths to victory. The scenario offers different approaches based on your campaign choices. Read your options carefully.
  • Every stat matters. Expect tests of all types. Balanced investigators have an edge.
  • Your campaign state determines difficulty. Trauma, Vengeance, supplies — everything accumulated across 7 scenarios affects the finale.
  • Use everything. Spend all resources, commit all cards. There's nothing after this.
  • Multiple endings exist. Some are bittersweet, some are triumphant. All are earned.
⚠️ Common mistakes across the whole campaign:
  • Killing Vengeance enemies — evade them. Every kill makes Scenario 7 harder.
  • Skipping supplies — Provisions and Rope pay for themselves multiple times over.
  • Bringing low-agility teams — traps test agility constantly. Will 5 doesn't help when you need Agi 3+.
  • Not bringing healing — trauma accumulates. Without healing, investigators die mid-campaign.
