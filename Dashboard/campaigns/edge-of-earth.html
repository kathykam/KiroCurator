📖 Edge of the Earth

Campaign guide — spoiler-free strategy tips

Premise: An Antarctic expedition goes wrong. Survive the ice, uncover ancient secrets, and face horrors from beyond the stars. Based on Lovecraft's "At the Mountains of Madness."
Players: 1–4 | Difficulty: Medium–Hard | Length: ~16–20 hours (5 main scenarios with sub-scenarios and interludes)
Key mechanics: Partner allies (expedition team members who travel with you), Tekeli-li weakness deck (shared madness cards), branching scenario structure, Frost tokens in chaos bag
XP available: ~40–55 total (generous — partners can die giving bonus XP)

Recommended Teams (3 players)

🃏 Team 1 (prebuilt — best): Zoey + Harvey + Agnes — Tekeli-li tests willpower when drawn — Zoey (Will 4), Harvey (Will 4), and Agnes (Will 5) all handle it. Harvey's Int 5 + card draw is better than Rex for the massive maps — consistency over burst speed across a long campaign. Harvey's draw means the team always has commits available for Frost-token-worsened tests. Zoey's resource generation funds expensive upgrades. Agnes anchors willpower and provides flexible spell damage.
Team 2: Zoey + Rex + Jacqueline — Jacqueline's token manipulation directly counters Frost tokens (she can avoid pulling them). Will 5 handles Tekeli-li. Zoey handles the heavy combat. Rex for speed. Very strong defensive team.
Team 3: Zoey + Harvey + Agnes — Harvey's card draw keeps everyone fueled across the long campaign. His Int 5 handles high-shroud Antarctic locations. Will 4 handles Tekeli-li. Agnes + Zoey cover combat and willpower.

Partner Allies — How They Work

You choose expedition team members at campaign start. They travel with you as story allies — free assets that provide bonuses. But they can be killed or go missing.

Key rules:
• Each investigator gets one partner. Partners have health/sanity and can be defeated.
• Dead partners give bonus XP but you lose their ability permanently.
• Partners provide stat boosts, soak, or special abilities. Choose partners that cover your investigator's weaknesses.
• Protect your partners. Their abilities compound over the campaign. Losing one early hurts.

Scenario 1: Ice and Death

Setting: Antarctic ice shelf. Your expedition has just arrived.
Primary challenge: Exploration + survival. Introduces the campaign's core mechanics.
Doom pressure: Moderate — weather creates time pressure
  • This scenario branches into sub-scenarios. Based on your choices, you'll play different follow-up scenarios. All paths are viable — choose based on your team's strengths.
  • Frost tokens enter the chaos bag. These are negative modifiers that accumulate over the campaign. Every Frost token makes future tests harder. Avoid gaining them when possible.
  • Tekeli-li cards are shared weaknesses. They shuffle into your deck and test willpower when drawn. High willpower investigators handle them better.
  • Explore the map thoroughly. Locations are face-down and revealed as you move. Some contain supplies or story assets that help later.
  • Partners are vulnerable. Enemies can attack your partners. Position your fighter to protect them.

Scenario 2: Fatal Mirage

Setting: A dreamlike space. Memories of your partners manifest as threats.
Primary challenge: Navigate a surreal map + protect partners from their own pasts
Doom pressure: Moderate
  • This scenario is about your partners. Their backstories create the threats. Partners you chose determine which enemies and locations appear.
  • The map is non-linear. Locations connect in unusual ways. Read connection symbols carefully — don't assume adjacency.
  • Enemies are tied to specific partners. Defeating them resolves that partner's story. Prioritize partners whose abilities you rely on most.
  • Willpower tests are frequent. The dreamlike setting tests willpower heavily. Agnes and Jacqueline excel.
  • This scenario can be replayed. It appears multiple times in the campaign with different partner memories each time.

Scenario 3: To the Forbidden Peaks

Setting: Mountain ascent. Climbing toward something ancient.
Primary challenge: Vertical movement — ascend the mountain while surviving hazards
Doom pressure: High — weather and enemies close in
  • Movement is vertical. You climb upward through locations. Falling back down wastes actions. Bring movement/climbing aids.
  • Weather hazards deal damage. Treacheries deal direct damage/horror from cold and altitude. Health and sanity soak are essential.
  • Enemies are tough. Antarctic creatures are strong fighters. Roland/Zoey need upgraded weapons by now.
  • Frost tokens accumulate. The chaos bag gets worse. Over-commit to important tests — you can't afford failures at altitude.
  • Supplies from earlier scenarios help. Items found during Ice and Death may provide bonuses here.

Scenario 4: City of the Elder Things

Setting: An ancient alien city beneath the ice.
Primary challenge: Massive exploration map + multiple objectives
Doom pressure: Moderate — but the map is huge
  • The map is enormous. Many locations to explore. Split up to cover ground but stay within reach of your fighter.
  • Multiple objectives compete for time. You can't do everything. Prioritize based on what your campaign needs.
  • Some enemies are not hostile. Not everything in the city wants to kill you. Pay attention to enemy keywords before attacking.
  • Investigation is primary. Rex and Harvey shine here — lots of clues across many locations. High shroud values in the alien city.
  • This scenario determines the finale's difficulty. What you accomplish here directly affects the final scenario. Be thorough.

Scenario 5: The Heart of Madness

Setting: Deep beneath the city. The source of everything.
Primary challenge: The finale — survival + multiple paths to victory
Doom pressure: Extreme
  • This is a two-part finale. Expect a long session. Both parts are intense.
  • Everything you've done matters. Partners alive, Frost tokens accumulated, objectives completed — all affect difficulty and available options.
  • Combat is heavy. The finale throws powerful enemies at you. Upgraded weapons and damage spells are essential.
  • Willpower remains critical. Tekeli-li cards and treacheries continue testing willpower. Low-will investigators may be overwhelmed.
  • Multiple endings based on your campaign path. All are narratively satisfying. Use everything you have — there's nothing after this.
⚠️ Common mistakes across the campaign:
  • Letting partners die for XP early — their abilities are worth more than the XP over 5 scenarios.
  • Ignoring Frost tokens — they compound. Every token you can avoid gaining saves you across the entire campaign.
  • Low willpower teams — Tekeli-li punishes Will 1-2 every time it's drawn. Bring at least 2 high-will investigators.
  • Not exploring thoroughly in Scenario 4 — what you find there determines the finale's difficulty.
