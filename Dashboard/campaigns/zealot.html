📖 Night of the Zealot

Campaign guide — spoiler-free strategy tips

Premise: Strange things stir in Arkham. A cult worships an ancient evil beneath your very home. Stop the ritual before the Devourer consumes everything.
Players: 1–4 | Difficulty: Easy–Medium | Length: ~3 hours (3 scenarios)
Key mechanics: Doom clock, cultist hunting, chaos bag introduction
XP available: ~10 total (4 + 4 + 2 from victory display)

Recommended Teams (3 players)

🃏 Team 1 (prebuilt — best): Roland + Rex + Agnes — Roland fights + gets clues on kill (perfect for Gathering's Ghoul Priest). Rex vacuums clues in Midnight Masks to find cultists fast. Agnes handles willpower treacheries and pings enemies with horror damage. Balanced coverage of all three scenarios' demands.
Team 2: Roland + Rex + Jacqueline — Jacqueline's token manipulation softens the chaos bag (which gets worse each scenario). Her willpower 5 handles treacheries that Rex and Roland struggle with. Slightly less damage output than Agnes.
Team 3: Zoey + Rex + Agnes — Zoey generates resources from engaging enemies (plenty of cultists and ghouls). Stronger combat than Roland but no clue-on-kill synergy. Better for Devourer Below's heavy combat.

Scenario 1: The Gathering

Setting: Your house. Something is in the basement.
Primary challenge: Clue gathering → boss fight (Ghoul Priest)
Doom pressure: Moderate — agenda gives you time if you're efficient

Strategy Tips

  • Rush the Study. The Study has 2 clues per investigator — clearing it advances the act. Don't waste actions exploring other rooms first.
  • Accept help when offered. An ally appears early — take her. She gives +1 combat vs Monsters and 1 bonus damage, making the boss fight dramatically easier.
  • Boss fight: fight or resign? Fighting the boss gives 2 XP (victory display). With 3 players it has ~10 health. If your team lacks combat, resigning is fine — you keep your investigators for the next scenario.
  • Hallway is a trap. The Hallway connects everything but has no clues. Don't end your turn there — Ghouls spawn and you waste actions moving.
  • Rotting Remains is the scariest treachery. Willpower test or take horror. Agnes loves this (triggers her ability). Others need willpower icons or cancellation.
⚠️ Common mistakes:
  • Splitting up too early — the boss needs focused damage from multiple investigators.
  • Ignoring rooms with clues you need to advance the act.
  • Burning your ally's damage on weak enemies instead of saving her for the boss.

Scenario 2: The Midnight Masks

Setting: Downtown Arkham at night. Cultists hide among the townsfolk.
Primary challenge: Find and interrogate/defeat as many cultists as possible before doom runs out
Doom pressure: HIGH — cultists add doom, agenda is tight

Strategy Tips

  • This is a race, not a puzzle. You won't get all 6 cultists on your first play. Getting 4 is good, 5 is great, 6 is exceptional.
  • Split up immediately. Each location has 1-2 clues that reveal cultist locations. Three investigators covering three locations = maximum information per round.
  • Resign timing matters. You can resign at any time at the starting location. Resign when doom is about to pop the agenda — don't let it advance. Cultists you've defeated/interrogated carry forward; ones still in play don't.
  • Prioritize high-doom cultists. Some cultists add doom when they enter play. Kill or parley them first before they accelerate the clock.
  • Rex is MVP here. His extra-clue ability means he finds cultist locations in half the actions. Send him to high-shroud locations.
  • Mysterious Chanting is the worst treachery — adds doom to an enemy. Cancel it if you can.
⚠️ Common mistakes:
  • Staying grouped — you need to cover ground fast, not fight together.
  • Fighting cultists you could Parley — some can be interrogated (intellect/willpower test) which is faster than combat.
  • Forgetting to resign — if the agenda advances, ALL remaining cultists escape. Resign one round early.

Scenario 3: The Devourer Below

Setting: The woods south of Arkham. Something ancient stirs.
Primary challenge: Navigate locations → disrupt a ritual OR face the final threat
Doom pressure: Moderate — but chaos bag is at its worst

Strategy Tips

  • Cultists you missed in Scenario 2 appear here. Every cultist that escaped Midnight Masks makes this scenario harder. This is why Scenario 2 matters so much.
  • The path is linear. You must move through several locations to reach the final site. Movement efficiency matters — don't backtrack.
  • You don't have to fight the final enemy. You can win by advancing the act (placing clues). With 3 players, the direct fight is extremely hard.
  • There are multiple valid endings. Some involve sacrifice, some involve brute force. Read your options carefully when they appear.
  • Chaos bag is brutal now. After two scenarios of adding tokens, expect -3 to -5 modifiers. Over-commit to important tests.
  • Bring movement cards. Shortcut, Elusive, Pathfinder — anything that saves actions moving through the woods.
⚠️ Common mistakes:
  • Trying to fight the final enemy head-on with a 3-player team — it has massive health. Advance the act instead.
  • Under-committing to tests — the chaos bag is much worse than Scenarios 1-2. Commit cards liberally.
  • Forgetting that woods locations are randomized — you may need to explore multiple paths.
