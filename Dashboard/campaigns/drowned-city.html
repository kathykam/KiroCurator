📖 The Drowned City

Campaign guide — spoiler-free strategy tips

Premise: Something stirs beneath Arkham. The city floods, ancient ruins surface, and a cosmic horror awakens. Tied to the Cthulhu mythos directly.
Players: 1–4 | Difficulty: Medium–Hard | Length: ~18–22 hours (9 scenarios)
Key mechanics: Flood levels (locations become submerged, changing their properties), Glyph puzzles (rune-matching for rewards), Artifact story assets (powerful items found during play), Alert/Patrol enemies
XP available: ~40–50 total across 9 scenarios

Recommended Teams (3 players)

🃏 Team 1 (prebuilt — best): Nathaniel + Rex + Agnes — Alert enemies deal damage when you FAIL attacks. Nathaniel's Combat 5 minimizes failures — he almost never triggers Alert. His event-based fighting doesn't provoke attacks of opportunity, making him ideal for aggressive enemies. Agnes (Will 5) handles cosmic treacheries that dominate the back half. Rex solves Glyph puzzles fast with his extra-clue ability. Nathaniel's weaknesses (Int 2, Will 3) are fully covered by his teammates.
Team 2: Zoey + Rex + Agnes — Zoey's Combat 4 + Will 4 + resources. More balanced than Nathaniel — she handles willpower tests herself. Slightly less combat output but more self-sufficient. Good if you want fewer team dependencies.
Team 3: Nathaniel + Rex + Jacqueline — Jacqueline's token manipulation helps Nathaniel's already-high combat become near-guaranteed. Her Will 5 covers cosmic treacheries. Rex for clues. Maximum combat reliability against Alert enemies.

Flood Levels — How They Work

Locations have flood levels that increase over time. As flood rises, locations change:

• Unflooded: Normal. No penalties.
• Partially flooded: Increased shroud or movement costs. Harder to investigate.
• Fully flooded: Location may become inaccessible or extremely dangerous. May deal damage to investigators there.

Strategy: Investigate locations BEFORE they flood. Prioritize low-lying locations first. Movement between flooded locations may cost extra actions.

Scenario 1: One Last Job

Setting: Arkham's underworld. Gang conflict.
Primary challenge: Navigate criminal factions + gather information
Doom pressure: Moderate
  • This is a noir-style opening. Investigation and social interaction dominate. Combat is present but not the focus.
  • Multiple factions compete. You interact with rival gangs. Your choices affect which allies and enemies appear later.
  • Intellect and combat both matter. You need clues to advance but enemies are aggressive. Balanced teams handle this best.
  • Introduces the campaign's tone. Darker and more grounded than other campaigns initially — the cosmic horror builds gradually.

Scenario 2: The Western Wall

Setting: Ancient underwater ruins. The first descent.
Primary challenge: Navigate treacherous paths + solve Glyph puzzles
Doom pressure: Moderate — flood timer creates urgency
  • Flood mechanics fully activate here. Locations flood over time. Investigate before the water rises.
  • Glyph puzzles appear. Matching rune symbols unlocks rewards (artifacts, story progression). Pay attention to symbols on locations and cards.
  • The path is dangerous. Treacherous locations test agility or deal damage. Movement isn't free — plan your route.
  • Deep One enemies appear. Aquatic enemies that are stronger in flooded locations. Fight them in unflooded areas when possible.

Scenario 3: The Drowned Quarter

Setting: Submerged ruins. An ancient barrier system.
Primary challenge: Reactivate ancient technology while surviving underwater threats
Doom pressure: High — the dome is collapsing
  • Objective-based scenario. You must interact with specific locations/objects to progress. Clues alone don't advance the act.
  • Enemies are tougher here. Underwater creatures are more dangerous. Upgraded weapons help.
  • Artifacts found here carry forward. Story assets you discover become permanent additions to your campaign. Search thoroughly.
  • Flood pressure is real. The collapsing environment creates a timer. Don't linger — accomplish objectives and move on.

Scenario 4: The Apiary

Setting: A biological horror — organic tunnels and parasitic threats.
Primary challenge: Survive a hostile environment + rescue objectives
Doom pressure: High — parasites spread
  • The environment itself is hostile. Locations deal damage or horror just for being there. Health and sanity soak are essential.
  • Parasitic enemies transform. Some enemies change form or attach to investigators. Dealing with them quickly prevents escalation.
  • Combat is primary. This is the most combat-heavy scenario so far. Roland/Zoey/Nathaniel need to be fully online.
  • Artifacts continue to appear. Glyph-marked items provide campaign-long benefits. Don't skip them.

Scenario 5: The Grand Vault

Setting: Deep within R'lyeh. Ancient machinery.
Primary challenge: Navigate shifting architecture + solve complex puzzles
Doom pressure: High — walls shift on a timer
  • The map shifts. Locations move or change connections. You can't plan a fixed route — adapt each round.
  • Glyph puzzles are more complex. Multiple runes must be matched in sequence. Intellect-heavy investigators shine.
  • Deadly mechanisms deal damage. Treacheries from the environment are hazard-typed. Agility or intellect tests to avoid.
  • This is a puzzle scenario. Combat is secondary to navigation and problem-solving. Rex and Harvey are more valuable than fighters here.

Scenario 6: Court of the Ancients

Setting: A towering structure. Ancient beings observe you.
Primary challenge: Ascend while dealing with massive threats
Doom pressure: High
  • Vertical movement returns. You ascend through the structure. Falling back wastes actions.
  • A massive enemy guards the area. It's extremely tough. You may need to bypass it rather than fight directly.
  • Willpower tests increase. The cosmic influence grows stronger. Low-willpower investigators suffer.
  • Artifacts from earlier scenarios may help. Story assets you've collected can provide shortcuts or bonuses here.

Scenario 7: Obsidian Canyons

Setting: Open alien landscape. Storms and flying threats.
Primary challenge: Exploration under hostile weather + aerial enemies
Doom pressure: High — storms close in
  • Weather is a mechanic. Storms affect movement and tests. Timing your actions around weather patterns matters.
  • Flying enemies are dangerous. They move unpredictably and hit hard. Combat readiness is essential.
  • The map is open. Multiple paths to objectives. Split up to cover ground but regroup for combat.
  • Agility tests for terrain. Navigating the canyons tests agility. High-agility investigators move faster.

Scenario 8: Sepulchre of the Sleeper

Setting: The resting place. Something ancient sleeps here.
Primary challenge: Interact with the environment before it's too late
Doom pressure: Extreme — awakening is imminent
  • This is the penultimate scenario. What you accomplish here directly determines the finale's difficulty.
  • Glyph puzzles reach their climax. All the rune knowledge you've gathered pays off. Artifacts provide bonuses.
  • Willpower is critical. The cosmic presence tests willpower heavily. Agnes and Jacqueline are essential.
  • Time is extremely tight. Every action must advance your objective. No exploration for its own sake.

Scenario 9: The Doom of Arkham

Setting: Arkham itself. The finale.
Primary challenge: Save the city — everything converges
Doom pressure: Maximum
  • Two-part finale. Expect a long, intense session. Both parts are brutal.
  • Everything you've done matters. Artifacts collected, Glyphs solved, allies saved — all affect your options and difficulty.
  • Combat is extreme. The final threats are the toughest in the campaign. Fully upgraded weapons and damage spells are mandatory.
  • Multiple paths to victory. Your campaign choices determine which endings are available. All require sacrifice.
  • Use everything. Spend all resources, commit all cards, activate all artifacts. This is the end.
⚠️ Common mistakes across the campaign:
  • Ignoring flood timing — investigate locations BEFORE they flood. Once submerged, shroud increases dramatically.
  • Skipping Glyph puzzles — artifacts from puzzles provide permanent campaign benefits. They're worth the actions.
  • Fighting Alert enemies without high combat — Alert means they hit you if you fail. Only attack with Combat 4+ or use events.
  • Not bringing combat upgrades for the back half — Scenarios 6-9 are extremely combat-heavy.
