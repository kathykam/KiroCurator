📖 The Path to Carcosa

Campaign guide — spoiler-free strategy tips

Premise: A mysterious play comes to Arkham. Wherever it's performed, madness follows. Investigate the truth behind the performance — if truth even exists anymore.
Players: 1–4 | Difficulty: Medium–Hard | Length: ~14–18 hours (8 scenarios)
Key mechanics: Hidden cards (secret information per player), Doubt/Conviction (campaign choices that branch the story), horror-heavy encounter deck, Haunted keyword
XP available: ~35–50 total across the campaign

Recommended Teams (3 players)

🃏 Team 1 (prebuilt — best): Zoey + Rex + Agnes — This campaign is horror-heavy (willpower tests constantly). Agnes's Will 5 anchors the team. Zoey's Will 4 means she handles Haunted locations and horror treacheries far better than Roland (Will 3) would across 8 scenarios. Rex's speed handles investigation-heavy early scenarios. Zoey covers combat which ramps in the back half, and her resource generation funds upgrades across the long campaign.
Team 2: Zoey + Harvey + Jacqueline — Maximum willpower coverage. Harvey (Will 4, Int 5) and Jacqueline (Will 5) both handle horror treacheries. Jacqueline's token manipulation helps Zoey land combat tests. Card draw from Harvey keeps everyone fueled.
Team 3: Zoey + Rex + Jacqueline — Jacqueline replaces Agnes for even stronger token manipulation. Will 5 + token control means the worsening chaos bag barely matters. Trade-off: less flexible damage than Agnes.

Scenario 1: Curtain Call

Setting: A theatre. The play is about to begin.
Primary challenge: Investigation + managing a growing horror threat
Doom pressure: Moderate
  • Investigate the theatre thoroughly. Clues advance the act. Multiple locations to search — split up early.
  • Horror is the primary damage type. Treacheries deal horror, not damage. Sanity soak (allies, Fearless) is more important than health soak here.
  • Haunted locations punish movement. When you enter a Haunted location, you must pass a willpower test or suffer a penalty. Plan your movement to minimize unnecessary location changes.
  • A persistent enemy may appear. It's tough to kill early. Evading is valid — focus on advancing the act.

Scenario 2: The Last King

Setting: A dinner party. The guests are... unusual.
Primary challenge: Social interaction (Parley) + information gathering
Doom pressure: Moderate — but guests add doom over time
  • Parley is the primary action. You interact with guests via Parley (intellect or willpower tests). High-stat investigators dominate.
  • Each guest gives different information. Talk to as many as possible. The information you gather affects the rest of the campaign.
  • Guests can become hostile. Have a fighter ready in case things go wrong, but prioritize Parley over combat.
  • This scenario sets up the Doubt/Conviction mechanic. Your choices here branch the campaign. There's no "wrong" choice — both paths are interesting.
  • Rex and Agnes excel. Rex's Int 4 and Agnes's Will 5 both pass Parley tests reliably.

Scenario 3: Echoes of the Past

Setting: A historical building. Searching for answers in the archives.
Primary challenge: Clue gathering under escalating pressure
Doom pressure: High — enemies and doom accelerate
  • Clue efficiency is critical. You need many clues across multiple floors. Rex's double-clue ability shines.
  • Enemies spawn and escalate. The longer you take, the more dangerous it gets. Speed > thoroughness.
  • Haunted locations continue. Willpower tests on entry. Plan routes to minimize backtracking through Haunted rooms.
  • Bring horror soak. The encounter deck continues to deal horror. Investigators with low sanity (5 or less) are at risk of defeat.

Scenario 4: The Unspeakable Oath

Setting: A place of confinement. You may not be who you think you are.
Primary challenge: Escape + investigation under hostile conditions
Doom pressure: High
  • You start in a bad position. Expect to begin without full access to your assets. Prioritize getting set up quickly.
  • Enemies are numerous and aggressive. Combat is more important here than in earlier Carcosa scenarios. Roland/Zoey need weapons ready.
  • Hidden cards come into play. You may have secret information that affects your options. Pay attention to what you're told privately.
  • Agility helps for escape. Some challenges can be bypassed with evade. Jenny and Winifred have an edge.

Scenario 5: A Phantom of Truth

Setting: City streets. Something follows you.
Primary challenge: Investigation while being pursued
Doom pressure: Moderate — but the pursuer creates constant tension
  • A powerful enemy stalks you. It moves toward you each round. You can't easily kill it — evade and stay ahead.
  • Investigate while running. You need clues from locations while keeping distance from the pursuer. Action efficiency is everything.
  • Movement cards are essential. Shortcut, Elusive, Pathfinder — anything that lets you investigate AND move in the same turn.
  • Your Doubt/Conviction choices affect this scenario. The campaign branches based on earlier decisions. Both paths are viable.

Scenario 6: The Pallid Mask

Setting: Underground. A labyrinth of passages.
Primary challenge: Navigate a randomized map + survive
Doom pressure: Moderate
  • The map is randomized. Locations are placed face-down and revealed as you explore. You can't plan a route in advance.
  • Dead ends are real. You may need to backtrack. Movement efficiency matters — don't over-commit to one direction.
  • Enemies lurk in the dark. Combat encounters are frequent. Keep your fighter close to the group.
  • Haunted keyword is everywhere. Almost every location is Haunted. High willpower is essential for the entire team.
  • Bring light sources / investigation tools. High shroud values in the dark. Flashlight, Magnifying Glass, or intellect boosts are critical.

Scenario 7: Black Stars Rise

Setting: A coastal location. Reality fractures.
Primary challenge: Race against doom + choose your path
Doom pressure: EXTREME — multiple doom sources
  • Doom comes from everywhere. Agenda, enemies, treacheries — all add doom. This is one of the tightest doom clocks in the game.
  • You must choose a path. The scenario branches. Both options are viable but require different approaches. Read carefully.
  • Speed is survival. Every wasted action brings you closer to failure. Prioritize act advancement above all else.
  • Cancellation is gold. Treacheries that add doom must be cancelled if possible. Any "cancel a treachery" effect is worth its weight.
  • This is the hardest scenario in the campaign for many groups. Don't be discouraged by a loss — it's notoriously tight.

Scenario 8: Dim Carcosa

Setting: Somewhere else entirely. The end of the path.
Primary challenge: The finale — everything you've built leads here
Doom pressure: High
  • Your Doubt/Conviction total determines the scenario's shape. The finale plays differently based on your campaign choices. Both versions are beatable.
  • Every stat matters. Expect tests of all four stats. Balanced teams have an advantage over hyper-specialists.
  • Horror is extreme. The encounter deck deals massive horror. Sanity soak and willpower are critical. Low-sanity investigators may be defeated by treacheries alone.
  • Use everything you have. This is the end — spend every resource, commit every card. There's no "saving for later."
  • Multiple endings exist. Your choices throughout the campaign determine which resolutions are available. All are narratively satisfying.
⚠️ Common mistakes:
  • Bringing low-willpower investigators — this campaign punishes Will 1-2 relentlessly from start to finish.
  • Ignoring horror soak — more investigators are defeated by horror than damage in Carcosa.
  • Hoarding resources in the finale — spend everything. There's no scenario after this one.
