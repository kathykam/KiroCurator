📖 The Dunwich Legacy

Campaign guide — spoiler-free strategy tips

Premise: Professors from Miskatonic University have gone missing. Your mentor asks you to investigate. Classic Lovecraft — the Dunwich Horror brought to the card game.
Players: 1–4 | Difficulty: Medium | Length: ~12–16 hours (8 scenarios)
Key mechanics: Exile (cards removed from game permanently), Beyond the Veil (damage for failed willpower), timed scenarios, ally NPCs at risk
XP available: ~35–45 total across the campaign

Recommended Teams (3 players)

🃏 Team 1 (prebuilt — best): Zoey + Rex + Jacqueline — Beyond the Veil tests willpower across 8 scenarios — Zoey's Will 4 passes where Roland's Will 3 fails. Jacqueline's token manipulation DIRECTLY helps Rex pass willpower tests he'd otherwise fail and counters the worsening chaos bag. Her Will 5 protects the whole team from Beyond the Veil. Rex handles ALL clues so Roland's flex investigation is wasted. Zoey's resource generation from engaging enemies funds upgrades across the long campaign.
Team 2: Zoey + Rex + Stella — Stella's fail-forward shines in timed scenarios — failed tests still give her benefits and trigger extra actions. Her Agi 4 dominates the Museum evasion scenario. Survivor recursion keeps her alive through trauma. Different feel: aggressive tempo, less spell-reliant than Agnes.
Team 3: Nathaniel + Harvey + Jacqueline — Completely different playstyle. Nathaniel's Combat 5 demolishes the back half. Harvey's Int 5 + card draw handles all clues and keeps the team fueled. Jacqueline's token manipulation counters Beyond the Veil AND helps Nathaniel land attacks. Brute force + consistency.

Scenario 1: Extracurricular Activity

Setting: Miskatonic University campus at night.
Primary challenge: Investigation + a dangerous enemy that patrols the map
Doom pressure: Moderate
  • A large enemy patrols between locations. You can fight it or avoid it — both are valid. Evading and focusing on clues is often faster.
  • Multiple buildings to explore. Split up to cover ground but stay within 1 move of your fighter in case the patrol catches someone.
  • Willpower matters early. Treacheries in this scenario test willpower. Agnes and Jacqueline shine; Rex and Roland need committed cards.
  • You can play Scenarios 1 and 2 in either order. This one is slightly easier to start with for most teams.

Scenario 2: The House Always Wins

Setting: A speakeasy with dangerous clientele.
Primary challenge: Social investigation that can turn violent fast
Doom pressure: Moderate — but things escalate
  • Start by gathering information. The early game is investigation-focused. Don't start fights you don't need to.
  • Things escalate suddenly. Be ready to pivot from clue-gathering to survival. Have weapons in hand before the midpoint.
  • Resources matter here. Jenny's economy shines. Expensive assets need to be down early.
  • An ally NPC can be saved or lost. Prioritize reaching them — their fate carries forward in the campaign.

Scenario 3: The Miskatonic Museum

Setting: A museum after hours. Something hunts you in the dark.
Primary challenge: Evasion + investigation under pursuit
Doom pressure: Low — but a relentless hunter creates constant pressure
  • A powerful enemy hunts you throughout. It's very hard to kill early. Evade it and keep moving — fighting wastes actions.
  • Agility matters. Evade tests are frequent. Winifred and Jenny have an edge here. Roland struggles — keep him away from the hunter.
  • Investigate efficiently. You need clues from multiple rooms. Rex's ability to grab 2 clues per action is huge for staying ahead of the hunter.
  • Don't dead-end yourself. Always have an escape route. If the hunter corners you in a room with one exit, you're in trouble.

Scenario 4: The Essex County Express

Setting: A train. It's not stopping.
Primary challenge: TIMED — locations disappear behind you. Move forward or die.
Doom pressure: EXTREME — the train cars vanish on a timer
  • This is the most famous scenario in the game. Pure speed. You must move forward through train cars, gathering clues to unlock the next one, before the rear cars are destroyed.
  • Every action counts. No wasted turns. If you spend an action doing anything other than investigating, moving, or dealing with immediate threats, you're falling behind.
  • Rex is MVP. His extra clue per success means you clear cars in half the time. Prioritize getting him to the front.
  • Bring movement cards. Shortcut, Elusive, anything that saves move actions. One saved action can mean the difference between life and death.
  • Enemies are distractions. Evade if possible. Only fight if an enemy is blocking your path forward. Every action spent fighting is an action not spent advancing.
⚠️ Common mistakes:
  • Staying in a car to "clear it fully" — move forward even if you leave clues behind. Survival > perfection.
  • Fighting every enemy — evade and run. The train doesn't care about your combat prowess.
  • Not having movement cards in your deck — this scenario punishes slow decks brutally.

Scenario 5: Blood on the Altar

Setting: A small village. Something is wrong with the locals.
Primary challenge: Find hidden objectives before time runs out — allies are at stake
Doom pressure: High — a countdown threatens NPCs you've collected
  • Your campaign allies are at risk. NPCs you've saved in earlier scenarios can be permanently lost here. Speed matters.
  • Locations hide what you need. You must investigate locations to find key objectives. Spread out and search fast.
  • Combat ramps up. Enemies are tougher than earlier scenarios. Roland/Zoey need upgraded weapons by now.
  • Willpower treacheries continue. Beyond the Veil (direct damage for failed willpower) is in the encounter deck. Low-willpower investigators take a beating.

Scenario 6: Undimensioned and Unseen

Setting: Open countryside. Massive invisible enemies.
Primary challenge: Unique combat — you need specific tools to even interact with the threats
Doom pressure: Moderate
  • Normal weapons don't work. The enemies in this scenario require a specific approach to damage. You'll gain access to special tools — use them.
  • Willpower and intellect are your combat stats here. Investigators with high willpower or intellect can contribute to "fighting" even if they're not traditional fighters.
  • Agnes is incredible here. Her willpower 5 directly contributes to the unique combat mechanic. She goes from support to primary damage dealer.
  • Spread out. Threats move between locations. You need coverage across the map to intercept them.

Scenario 7: Where Doom Awaits

Setting: A hill. Reality is thinning.
Primary challenge: Ascending through increasingly dangerous locations
Doom pressure: High
  • The chaos bag is at its worst. Expect brutal modifiers. Over-commit to every important test.
  • Locations get harder as you ascend. Higher shroud, nastier enemies. Save your best cards and resources for the top.
  • Beyond the Veil is still here. Willpower failures deal direct damage. Agnes and Jacqueline handle this; Rex and Roland need willpower commits.
  • This is the gateway to the finale. Make sure your team is fully upgraded before attempting this. If you're under-powered, the final scenario will be brutal.

Scenario 8: Lost in Time and Space

Setting: Beyond reality. Nothing makes sense.
Primary challenge: Navigate an unpredictable map + survive the finale
Doom pressure: High
  • Locations are unstable. The map shifts and changes. You can't plan a fixed route — adapt on the fly.
  • Every stat gets tested. Willpower, intellect, combat, agility — all matter. Balanced investigators (Jenny, Pete) have an edge over specialists.
  • Bring cancellation. Treacheries here are devastating. Any card that cancels or avoids encounter cards is gold.
  • Stick together. Unlike earlier scenarios, splitting up here is dangerous. The map can separate you permanently.
  • Don't panic about the chaos bag. It's terrible, but you've had 7 scenarios of XP to build your deck. Trust your upgrades.
⚠️ Common mistakes:
  • Splitting up — the shifting map can strand investigators alone with no way to regroup.
  • Hoarding resources for "later" — there is no later. Spend everything to advance.
  • Giving up after a bad round — the scenario is winnable even after setbacks. Keep pushing.
