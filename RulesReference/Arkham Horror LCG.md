# Arkham Horror: The Card Game — Rules Reference

**Source:** Official FFG Learn to Play (Feb 2025) + Rules Reference + FAQ v2.5 (Feb 2026)
**PDF Downloads:** https://www.fantasyflightgames.com/en/products/arkham-horror-the-card-game/ (Support section)

---

## Game Overview

Cooperative LCG, 1–4 players. Investigators explore locations, gather clues, advance the act deck, and try to reach a favorable resolution before the agenda deck advances.

---

## Round Structure

Each round has 4 phases (skip Mythos phase on round 1):

1. **Mythos Phase**
2. **Investigation Phase**
3. **Enemy Phase**
4. **Upkeep Phase**

---

## Mythos Phase

1. Place 1 doom on the current agenda.
2. Check doom threshold: if total doom in play ≥ threshold, advance the agenda (discard ALL doom from play).
3. Each investigator draws 1 encounter card (in player order) and resolves it.

⚠️ Common mistake: Doom on ALL cards in play counts toward the threshold, not just doom on the agenda card.

⚠️ Common mistake: When the agenda advances, ALL doom in play is discarded (from the agenda AND from other cards like enemies or assets).

---

## Investigation Phase

Investigators take turns in any order (group decides). Each investigator gets **3 actions** per turn.

### Available Actions
- **Draw** — Draw 1 card from your deck.
- **Resource** — Gain 1 resource.
- **Play** — Play an asset or event from hand (pay resource cost).
- **Activate** — Use an →Action ability on a card in play.
- **Move** — Move to a connecting location.
- **Investigate** — Test intellect vs. location's shroud; success = discover 1 clue.
- **Engage** — Pull an enemy at your location into your threat area.
- **Evade** — Test agility vs. enemy's evade value; success = exhaust enemy, break engagement.
- **Fight** — Test combat vs. enemy's fight value; success = deal 1 damage (unless modified).

### Attacks of Opportunity
If you are engaged with a ready enemy and take any action OTHER than Fight, Evade, or an ability with Parley/Resign designator, each ready engaged enemy attacks you.

⚠️ Common mistake: Playing a card (even a weapon) while engaged triggers attacks of opportunity. Plan ahead!

⚠️ Common mistake: Moving away from an engaged enemy triggers an attack of opportunity. Evade first, then move.

---

## Enemy Phase

1. **Hunter enemies move** — Each ready, unengaged hunter enemy moves one location toward the nearest investigator. If already at a location with an investigator, it does not move.
2. **Enemies attack** — Each ready, engaged enemy attacks its engaged investigator (deal damage AND horror simultaneously). Then exhaust the enemy.

⚠️ Common mistake: Exhausted enemies do NOT attack during the enemy phase. Evading is powerful!

---

## Upkeep Phase

1. Flip all investigator mini-cards faceup.
2. Ready all exhausted cards. Unengaged enemies that ready at an investigator's location engage.
3. Each investigator draws 1 card and gains 1 resource.
4. Discard down to 8 cards in hand.

⚠️ Common mistake: Enemies ready during upkeep. An evaded enemy will re-engage you at step 2 if still at your location.

---

## Skill Tests

### Sequence
1. Determine skill type and difficulty.
2. Commit cards (matching icons or wild ?) from hand. Other investigators at same location may commit 1 card each.
3. Reveal chaos token from bag.
4. Apply modifier (or resolve symbol effect from reference card).
5. Determine success/failure: modified skill ≥ difficulty = success.
6. Apply results.
7. Discard committed cards; return token to bag.

### Chaos Token Symbols
- **Skull, Cultist, Tablet, Elder Thing** — See scenario reference card.
- **Elder Sign** — See your investigator card ability.
- **Auto-fail** — Automatic failure regardless of skill value.

⚠️ Common mistake: You commit cards BEFORE revealing the chaos token. You cannot wait to see the token first.

⚠️ Common mistake: Other investigators at the SAME LOCATION can commit 1 card each to help. They don't need to be engaged with the same enemy.

---

## Locations

- Enter play **unrevealed** (keyhole side up).
- When an investigator first enters → reveal it and place clues equal to its clue value (× number of investigators if per-investigator symbol shown).
- Connections shown by matching icons at bottom of location cards.

---

## Enemies & Engagement

- A ready, unengaged enemy at your location **automatically engages** you.
- Engaged enemies are in your **threat area** and move with you.
- An enemy engages when: it spawns ready at your location, moves to your location, you move to its location, or it readies at your location.

### Prey
Some enemies have "Prey —" which determines who they engage if multiple investigators are present.

---

## Damage & Horror

- **Damage** → placed on investigator card or assets with health.
- **Horror** → placed on investigator card or assets with sanity.
- Card with damage ≥ health OR horror ≥ sanity = **defeated**.
- Defeated investigator = **eliminated** from scenario.

You choose how to distribute incoming damage/horror among your investigator and eligible assets.

⚠️ Common mistake: You CAN assign damage/horror to assets to protect your investigator. Allies are great damage/horror sponges.

---

## Slots

Each investigator has:
- 1 Ally slot
- 1 Body slot
- 1 Accessory slot
- 2 Hand slots
- 2 Arcane slots

To play an asset when you lack the slot, discard an existing asset in that slot first.

---

## Advancing Act & Agenda

### Act Deck (investigators' progress)
- Spend clues (as a group) equal to the threshold shown. Does NOT cost an action. Can be done during any investigator's turn.
- If "Objective —" text is present, follow those instructions instead/additionally.

### Agenda Deck (Mythos progress)
- Advances only during Mythos phase step 2 (unless a card says otherwise).
- When it advances: discard ALL doom in play, flip agenda, follow instructions, remove it, next agenda becomes active.

---

## Key Timing Rules

- **"Fast"** cards don't cost an action to play. They have a specified timing window.
- **"Forced"** abilities trigger automatically when their condition is met.
- **"Revelation"** abilities trigger immediately when the card is drawn.
- **"When"** vs **"After"**: "When" interrupts (before resolution); "After" is reactive (after resolution).
- **The Grim Rule**: If unsure about a rules conflict, resolve it in the worst way for the investigators.

---

## Weakness Cards

- **Revelation** weakness: resolve immediately when drawn (from deck or encounter deck).
- Weakness drawn during upkeep draw: resolve it, does NOT count as your upkeep draw (draw again? — No, it does count. You just must resolve it).

⚠️ Common mistake: Drawing a weakness during your upkeep draw DOES count as your card draw for that phase. You don't draw again.

---

## Campaign Rules (Brief)

- After each scenario: earn XP from victory display + scenario resolution.
- Spend XP to upgrade cards (cost = difference in levels).
- Trauma carries between scenarios (physical trauma = start with damage; mental trauma = start with horror).
- Defeated investigators gain 1 physical trauma (if health) or 1 mental trauma (if sanity).

---

## Commonly Confused Rules

1. **Doom removal on agenda advance** — ALL doom in play is removed, not just from the agenda.
2. **Per investigator clues** — Multiply by number of investigators who STARTED the scenario (not currently alive).
3. **Attacks of opportunity** — Only from READY engaged enemies. Exhausted enemies don't AoO.
4. **Evade only breaks engagement** — The enemy stays at the location. It will re-engage during upkeep if still there.
5. **"Cannot"** — Always takes precedence over "can" or "may."
6. **Surge** — Draw another encounter card after resolving this one.
7. **Peril** — Only you can commit cards or trigger abilities during this skill test.
8. **Victory X** — When defeated, goes to victory display (shared XP pile), not discard.
9. **Aloof** — Does not automatically engage. You must use the Engage action.
10. **Massive** — Engaged with all investigators at its location simultaneously.
