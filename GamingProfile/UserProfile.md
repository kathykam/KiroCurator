# Board Gamer Profile

## Two Pillars of My Gaming Personality

### 1. The "Adventurer" — Cooperative & Dramatic

> "If it is Cooperative, I need DRAMA."

My dominant profile for cooperative games. I want to be a unique character in a high-stakes story. My core drive is **Narrative Agency** — a game that delivers the deep narrative of Arkham Horror LCG with the action/simplicity of Cthulhu: Death May Die, and no app.

I want **immersion** and **tension**.

| | Detail |
|---|---|
| ✅ Good Progression (Agency) | Arkham Horror LCG (buying new cards for reliable options), Cthulhu: Death May Die (leveling up on the insanity track) |
| ❌ Bad Progression (Chaos) | Mechs vs. Minions (chaotic programming that removes control) |
| ❌ Co-op Math Puzzles | Dry efficiency equations without narrative arc (e.g., Spirit Island). No spreadsheets with a theme. |
| ❌ Grid/Hex Combat | Grid movement and line-of-sight calculation for combat (e.g., Gloomhaven). Fine for puzzle games. |

**Benchmarks:** Arkham Horror LCG, Cthulhu: Death May Die, Battlestar Galactica, Nemesis

---

### 2. The "Competitive Architect" — Optimization & Growth

> "If it is Competitive, I need OPTIMIZATION and POSITIVE PROGRESS."

My dominant profile for competitive games. I want to start with nothing and build a powerful, efficient machine that outpaces the competition. These are "Multiplayer Solitaire with a Race" — compare engines, not destroy them.

I want clean, high-agency logic puzzles where I turn "Opportunity Luck" (a random card market) into a reliable strategy. I want to **spiral up** (get stronger every turn), not struggle to stay flat. Simple, satisfying turns (draft a tile, roll dice, place a token) with easy, straightforward scoring.

| | Detail |
|---|---|
| ❌ Negative Progress & Maintenance | Spending turns fixing mistakes or feeding workers feels like work. |

**Benchmarks:** Harmonies, Space Base, Wingspan, Splendor

## Key Principle: Luck vs. Chaos

> The single most important filter for recommendations.

### ✅ "Opportunity Luck" (High-Agency, Fun)

Randomness that presents a new, interesting puzzle for me to solve. I am always in 100% control of the strategic decision I make in response.

**Benchmarks:** Space Base dice rolls, Cthulhu: Death May Die dice, Azul tile draws

### ❌ "Systemic Chaos" (Low-Agency, Frustrating)

Randomness that invalidates a strategic plan or removes my agency.

**Benchmark:** Mechs vs. Minions — the core mechanic is chaotic, not empowering.

### Deck-Building

- ✅ In-game progression (e.g., Aeon's End)
- ✅ Campaign progression (e.g., Arkham Horror LCG)
- ❌ Pre-game lifestyle/competitive construction (e.g., Magic: The Gathering)

### Survival Grind

- ❌ Hard "No" (Benchmark: 7th Continent)
- ✅ "Survival Puzzle" is fine (e.g., Forbidden Desert)

### Fiddliness — The CPU Rule

| | Detail |
|---|---|
| ❌ Per-Turn Accounting | Calculating enemy stats, movement, initiative, or status effects every turn (e.g., Gloomhaven monster AI, Mice & Mystics initiative). I want to play my character, not run the game engine. |
| ✅ Round-Based Maintenance | Upkeep phases once per round are acceptable (e.g., Mythos phase in Eldritch Horror or Cthulhu: Death May Die). |

> Exception: If a game requires per-turn accounting, I will play it only if a digital version handles the administration (e.g., Gloomhaven Digital).

### Limited Communication & "Mind Reading"

- ❌ Abstract "Vibes" — games requiring "syncing minds" through silence or vague imagery. No agency. *(Avoid: Mysterium, The Crew, Hanabi, The Mind)*
- ✅ Linguistic/Social — deduction based on language and word association. *(Keep: Codenames)*

### Logical Deduction vs. Narrative Investigation

- ❌ "Math/Grid Puzzles" — pure deduction that feels like solving a math problem or filling a spreadsheet. *(Avoid: Turing Machine, The Search for Planet X, Deception: Murder in Hong Kong, Mind MGMT)*
- ✅ "Detective Stories" — deduction grounded in tangible evidence, story context, or visual searching. "Who did it?" not "Which number is it?" *(Keep: Sherlock Holmes Consulting Detective, MicroMacro: Crime City)*

## Curation Rules & Exceptions

### The "Gold Standard" Host Library

One game per major mechanism for hosting game nights. **Do not recommend selling these**, even if competitive or low-interaction. Includes: Puerto Rico, Terra Mystica, Chinatown, Dominion, etc.

Also includes effectively irreplaceable games:
- **Out of Print / Bankrupt Publisher** — mechanical classics that can't be easily rebought (e.g., Spirit Island)
- **High-Value Editions** — classics with sentimental value or rare components (e.g., plastic version of Acquire)

### The "Family" Collection

Games kept for family game nights including younger nieces and nephews. **Not to be sold.** (e.g., Small World, Sheriff of Nottingham, The Resistance)

### The "Extended Family" Tier

Sub-category for youngest relatives (Nieces & Nephews), distinct from the teenager "Family" list. Extremely simple rules are acceptable here. (e.g., Labyrinth)

### Hard "Keep" Categories

- Most 2-player games
- Most cooperative games

---

## Strict Rubric for New RPG/Campaign Games

Non-negotiable requirements:

1. ❌ No apps
2. ❌ No pre-game deck construction (in-game is fine)
3. ❌ No survival grind
4. ✅ Must have low fiddliness and straightforward action economy
