Roleplay Games
I enjoy roleplaying as a character solving problems making strategic choices. Focus on what I love about the games I love. This is the rubic for games ONLY for “Roleplaying Games” genre:
PRIORITY FILTERS (ABSOLUTE NON-NEGOTIABLES - GAMES MUST MEET ALL OF THESE FIRST AND FOREMOST):
NO APPS. NONE. EVER. (STRICTLY ANALOG ONLY): This is non-negotiable due to practical reasons:
No internet dependency for any part of the game.
No requirement to use a full laptop.
No "sacrificing" a personal phone for the entire table during gameplay.
NO PRE-GAME DECK BUILDING: Zero deck construction before playing. In-game deck building is fine.
GRIND MECHANICS nuance: 
BAD Grind (The Hindrance):
Definition: Mechanics where resource upkeep acts as an arbitrary "tax" or interruption that stops me from doing the part of the game I actually enjoy (e.g., Exploration or Narrative).
Benchmark Dislike: The 7th Continent. The hunting/stamina mechanic interrupts the exploration I love and forces me into a repetitive loop just to resume the "real" game.
Action: Avoid these games unless the exploration/story payoff is massive, in which case I will "Homebrew/House Rule" the grind away. 
ACCEPTABLE Grind (The Core Puzzle):
Definition: Mechanics where the survival struggle is the primary strategic loop. In these games, managing the threat of death is the puzzle itself, not a distraction from it.
Benchmark Like: Robinson Crusoe, Sleeping Gods. Here, the struggle to survive is the "Adventure," so the difficulty feels thematic and purposeful rather than annoying.
NO HEAVY IN-GAME SETUP OR FIDDLINESS (MUST BE LIGHT & QUICK TO PLAY): This is critical.
Minimal management of many small tokens or components during active gameplay. No constant micro-management of individual monster health, initiative, or character card management that involves frequent shuffling/discarding/retrieving. Okay with upfront setup that is consolidated (e.g., pulling a specific set of cards/tiles once, or modular boards to set up, then leaving them) even if it's substantial, especially if a friend handles it. Dislikes constant, ongoing fiddliness. (e.g. Mice and Mystics and Gloomhaven)
NO COMPLICATED ACTION ECONOMY OR TRACKING. What you do in your turn must be clear and straightforward, with no complex turn order (e.g., like Cthulhu: Death May Die, Arkham Horror LCG, or TIME Stories; not like Mice and Mystics' complicated action economy/tracking).
Not "thinky," overly long, or complex to manage (e.g., definitely not like Gloomhaven).
NO COMPLEX ACTION OPTIMIZATION PUZZLES OR OPAQUE TRACKING
What you do in your turn must be clear and straightforward. Dislikes intricate, "thinky" action systems where each turn is a complex puzzle to optimize (e.g., Gloomhaven's hand management, Roll Player Adventures' detailed dice manipulation chains). Okay with a clear action point system or narrative choices (like TIME Stories' TU optimization for discovery). Turn order must be simple.
NOT OVERLY PUNISHING OR UNFORGIVING:  Dislikes games where small mistakes lead to harsh, game-ending failures that feel like a wasted grind (e.g., Gloomhaven's scenario fails forcing replays of tactical combat). Okay with narrative consequences for failure, puzzle resets for optimization, or character defeat that leads to continued story progression (e.g., TIME Stories' runs, AH:LCG's trauma/story adaptation, C:DMD's death leading to madness powers).
No HOMEBREW or fan made solutions: Let’s stick the what’s published.
The "Narrative Cohesion" Filter
Rule: REJECT narrative games that rely on "random vignettes" or disconnected story paragraphs.
Reasoning: The "Adventurer" seeks agency and consequential progression. Random story beats that lack connection to the previous choice feel like "Systemic Chaos" rather than a story.
Application:  This is why Above and Below is a "Sell" (random paragraphs) while Arkham Horror LCG is a "Love" (evolving story).

CORE PREFERENCES (After Priority Filters Are Applied - What You ARE Looking For):
You are seeking cooperative, narrative-driven board games where the active role-play of distinct characters solving problems within a story is paramount.
You want to feel like your specific character is making their unique mechanical contribution to the unfolding plot and challenges.
The narrative should be direct, not merely abstract or emergent from mechanics.
You enjoy "collecting stuff" (tangible items, skills, equipment cards) that improves control and agency and not collecting for collection sake that directly enhances your character's abilities and options during gameplay.

In-game component management at the level of Eldritch Horror (multiple boards, various decks, character sheets with tracking, monster tokens, gates) and Cthulhu: Death May Die (character dashboards, monster figures with health pegs, dynamic boss phases) is perfectly fine.

NOT ACCEPTABLE: Overly intricate, "thinky" action systems where each turn feels like a complex puzzle to optimize (e.g., Gloomhaven's hand management). Turn order must be simple (e.g., player turns or clear phases, not constantly changing based on complex factors).

ACCEPTABLE: Games can be substantial in length (like Eldritch Horror, though perhaps less sprawl). Complexity should be manageable, allowing for engaged gameplay without constant rulebook consultation (e.g., Eldritch Horror and C:DMD are fine).
NOT ACCEPTABLE: "Thinky," overly long, or complex to manage (e.g., Gloomhaven, Spirit Island).
Punishment/Forgiveness:
ACCEPTABLE: Narrative consequences for failure, puzzle resets for optimization, or character defeat that leads to continued story progression (e.g., TIME Stories' runs, AH:LCG's trauma/story adaptation, C:DMD's death leading to madness powers).
